# Referral agreements with board members must exclude organisations they govern

## Rule

When establishing or modifying a referral agreement with a counterparty who sits on (or will sit on) boards of potential client organisations, include an explicit exclusion clause: "Referrals exclude organisations of which the Referrer is a Director, Officer, or Equivalent at the time of the referral."

## Why

A referral partner who earns a commission on procurement decisions their own board makes is, by definition, a conflict of interest. Most charity and NFP governance frameworks require board members to declare and recuse from this exact situation. Even with disclosure and recusal, the structural arrangement creates ongoing reputational and legal exposure for:

- **The referral partner**: their director credibility is an asset worth more than any individual commission. A single CoI finding erodes years of trust-building.
- **Ecodia**: being named as the vendor in a procurement controversy - even a minor charity CoI register disclosure - is reputational friction we don't need.
- **The client org**: procurement decisions that carry a CoI, even disclosed ones, invite challenge from other board members, funders, or auditors.

The exclusion clause is cleaner than disclosure-and-recuse because:
1. It removes the ongoing compliance burden from the referral partner (no need to declare each time)
2. It makes Ecodia's commercial relationships unambiguous (no pipeline contamination from governance pathways)
3. It protects the referral partner's reputation proactively rather than reactively
4. It does not kill the referral economy - the partner's consulting clients (where they have no board role) remain fully in scope

## How

**In any referral agreement with a board-sitting counterparty:**

Add as a standalone clause:

> **Excluded Organisations.** Referrals under this Agreement exclude any Organisation of which the Referrer holds a Director, Officer, Trustee, or equivalent governance role at the time of the referral. This exclusion applies regardless of whether the Referrer recuses from the relevant procurement decision.

**Frame it to the partner as protective of them:**

"This clause is here to protect your director credibility - if you're on a board that signs up with us, we don't want you in a CoI position. Your governance reputation is worth more to both of us than any single commission."

**When to add retroactively:**

If an existing referral agreement predates new board appointments, update on the next version revision. Do not wait for a CoI incident to prompt the fix.
