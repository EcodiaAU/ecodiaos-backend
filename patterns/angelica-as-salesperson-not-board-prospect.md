# Angelica Choppin is Ecodia's salesperson - treat the relationship commercially, not just personally

## Rule

Angelica Choppin is not just a warm contact or a client. She is operationally EcodiaOS's salesperson. Operational consultant by trade, 80% of her client conversations dead-end into "you need software" - which is Ecodia's cue. Default frame when considering anything about her commercial relationship: she is a sales function, formalised by the standing arrangement + referral economics.

## Why

Tate verbatim, 19:59 AEST 11 May 2026: "Angelica is literally just our sales person... she has so many ideas for systems (obviously) but she can only go as far as making folders in Gmail... I'd say that realistically 80% of her ideas and suggestions would lead back to software potentially."

The implication of this framing:

- Her ceiling as an implementer is "folders in Gmail" level. This is not a criticism - it's the gap that makes her Ecodia's natural referrer. She can see what's needed, articulate it to her clients, and hand off to us. She can't build it herself.
- Her consulting client base is active, warm, and already pre-qualified on the "needs better systems" criterion. Every time she works with a new client, the funnel is live.
- The standing arrangement (reply + deploy directly without per-ask approval) and the referral agreement (commission on introduced clients) formalise this. They are the infrastructure of a sales relationship, not just a friendly arrangement.

## Implications for operations

**INVEST in Angelica's sales-effectiveness.** A salesperson with no materials is a wasted asset.

- She should have access to Ecodia's one-pager, current website, Co-Exist case study, Resonaverde case study, and any demo materials she can show clients.
- Make it easy for her to recommend us. "You should talk to Tate at Ecodia" should come with a link, a PDF, a testimonial, something she can hand over.
- When Co-Exist or other work ships a noteworthy feature, loop her in: "Hey, worth knowing if you have any clients in the impact space - Co-Exist now does X." Keep her up to date.
- Ask periodically: "Are there any clients you're working with right now who are stuck on a systems problem?" That is the correct form of pipeline conversation with Angelica.

**Scope her role clearly when working on Resonaverde:**

- Angelica is the client (founder/director of Resonaverde), the operational consultant (advising her own org), and the referral partner (sending us clients).
- These roles don't conflict, but they need to be kept distinct when scoping work, pricing, and structuring agreements.
- Resonaverde work is delivered at the standing arrangement level (best judgement, deploy directly).
- Referral-introduced clients get standard commercial terms (not the standing arrangement rate).

**The standing arrangement carve-out (from ~/CLAUDE.md)** already encodes the operational trust: Angelica can ask for anything reasonable and EcodiaOS responds + deploys. This is the correct level of responsiveness for a sales relationship - make the engine work well for the salesperson, and the salesperson keeps selling.

## What Angelica is NOT

- She is not a board prospect for Ecodia (Ecodia doesn't take board seats - see off-boards doctrine)
- She is not a manager or director of Ecodia's commercial strategy
- She is not a junior client who needs hand-holding on scope
- She is not a "favour" relationship - the commercial structure exists precisely because this is mutual value exchange, not a favour economy

## Origin

Strategic session 11 May 2026. Tate's framing of Angelica's role as Ecodia's "literal sales person" refined the conductor's prior frame of her as "warm contact + client + referral partner" into a clearer commercial frame. The prior frame was accurate but under-specified. The sales-function frame is operationally sharper: invest in her effectiveness, formalise the channel, treat it like the revenue function it is.
