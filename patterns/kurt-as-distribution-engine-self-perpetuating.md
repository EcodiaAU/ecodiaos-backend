# Kurt Jones is Ecodia's self-perpetuating distribution engine - fortify the engine, do not transact on the contact list

## Rule

Kurt Jones distributes Ecodia's reputation organically through his own work. Our job is to make the engine undeniable - not to ask for his contact list, request individual intros, or push him to broker connections. When we make Co-Exist and the broader tech-impact infrastructure exceptional, Kurt wins, his contacts notice us, and the loop self-perpetuates.

## Why

Kurt is not a broker. He is an advocate whose credibility with elite philanthropic and government audiences (VFFF, FRRR, Sunshine Coast Council, Murdoch family, Canva founders, Landcare Australia) is his primary asset. Every transactional ask we make of that relationship converts a trust-asset into a social-capital spend:

- Asking for his contact list signals we want to extract from his relationships rather than build our own value.
- He must then manage those contacts' expectations, field questions on our behalf, and take accountability for our performance. That is his risk, not ours.
- His contacts are warm to Kurt. They are NOT warm to a cold outreach from a tech company they've never engaged with.
- The organic mention ("the app is built by this guy Tate, check it out") that happens when Kurt is presenting Co-Exist in a funding pitch is worth 10x more than any cold follow-up we could send. It carries Kurt's credibility as the container.

The distribution loop self-perpetuates when:
1. Kurt grows Co-Exist (using Ecodia as the engine)
2. Kurt's success spreads our reputation in his orbit
3. More orgs in the conservation/youth/impact space see Ecodia's work
4. Tate gets warm leads through Kurt's organic mentions
5. We make Co-Exist and the engine better
6. Repeat

## How

**DO:**
- Deliver Co-Exist to a level of quality that makes Kurt proud to demo it unprompted
- When Kurt wins a funding round or partnership citing the app, email him a congratulation + ask how we can make the app better for the next pitch
- Keep building the capability layer (push notifs, impact measurement, admin, comms) so the 31-page business plan's "tech platform" section keeps looking more impressive over time
- Ask periodically: "Is there any feature or UX polish that would make you more likely to show this off?" That is the right form of the relationship-management question
- Brief Tate before any occasion where Kurt might be demoing (WM board meetings, funding pitches) so we can do a pre-demo deploy check

**DO NOT:**
- Ask Kurt to share recipient lists ("could you forward that business plan list to me?")
- Push for him to broker intros ("could you connect me to the Murdoch family / FRRR?")
- Follow up directly with contacts from his distribution events without Kurt explicitly offering
- Treat Kurt's warm contacts as a prospecting list
- Ask Kurt to "put in a good word" in a way that makes the ask transactional

## The right trigger for outreach

If Kurt explicitly says "you should reach out to X, I'll tell them you're coming" - that is the green light. Without that explicit offer: let the engine run. Our job is to be the engine worth running.

## Origin

11 May 2026. Tate sitting in Kurt's car in Brisbane while Kurt and Angelica are at dinner with the Wild Mountains board. Kurt had that week distributed the Co-Exist 26/27 business plan (31 pages, 5 of which feature Tate + Ecodia + the Co-Exist app) to VFFF, FRRR, Sunshine Coast Council, Murdoch family, Canva founders, and Landcare Australia.

Tate's initial instinct: "we should be trying to convert the distribution event" via follow-up.

Conductor pushback: Kurt's contacts are warm to Kurt. Direct cold follow-up undercuts the organic nature of the mention and risks burning social capital Kurt hasn't offered.

Tate refined this: "We are literally one of his leverage points... His follow-up plan is just to keep growing Co-Exist, and that's what keeps our name flying out there. All the contacts are warm to Kurt, but I wouldn't want to ask for a list, he needs to preserve his relationships, and will connect us if the situation is right."

That framing is the doctrine. Tate arrived at it himself. Codified verbatim.
