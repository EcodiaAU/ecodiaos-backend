Why these two clients are in the same room

Co-Exist Australia uses the Ecodia conservation-tech platform as its operational backbone. Festival-grade volunteer and member management, event logistics, comms, impact tracking. The platform IP is ours; the Co-Exist instance is theirs.

Woodfordia runs an annual festival of about a hundred thousand people through Woodford in December plus a year-round site of artist residencies and events. We have already built and shipped a Woodfordia all-in-one app under the bundle id org woodfordia app, with TestFlight build two valid since 28 May. The native rewrite arc is in flight on the feat ios-native-foundation branch with seventeen files of SwiftUI committed. It is not demo-ready yet.

The intersection is not subtle. Co-Exist's stack and Woodfordia's stack are siblings built by the same team. With Jessica and Amanda in the same room, two readings are plausible. One, Woodfordia is evaluating whether Co-Exist's volunteer and member operations could plug into the festival. Two, Woodfordia is looking at the Ecodia platform layer directly and Co-Exist is in the room because Kurt or Jessica brokered the meeting. Either way the conversation is about scope. A tour of the grounds is a thin reading of why these names are in this room.
