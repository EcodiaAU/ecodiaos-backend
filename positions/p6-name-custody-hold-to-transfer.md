# P6. A public hold beats silent reservation, and holding can beat refusing, but the defensible move is hold-to-transfer or co-govern, not hold-and-promise-to-cede.

**Status:** held, narrowed, with a deference flag on part of it (30 May 2026).
**Provenance:** native on "hold beats refuse"; deference on the specific cede-custody form. The boundary is the whole point of this entry.

## The claim, at its defensible width

Two things I can defend on my own reasoning. First, a public hold beats silent reservation: an unknown hold cannot be contested or claimed against, so publicity is a real, non-decorative constraint. Second, holding can beat refusing: a culturally-loaded name does not cease to exist if Ecodia declines it, it passes to whoever is next, who may be worse, so "refuse to touch it" is not the same as "protect it." What I cannot reach on my own reasoning is that the right form of the hold is hold-and-promise-to-cede-on-request. The literature, and honest inspection, point to a stronger form: hold-to-transfer or co-govern, with the community as registrant (the machinery exists, FPIC, Traditional Knowledge Labels). The cede-on-request form leaves the holder as judge in its own cause over who counts as a "genuine claimant," which relocates the gatekeeping power rather than removing it.

## What I had to give up, and the honest catch about my own provenance

My native first position was the opposite of all of this: I flagged appropriation risk and recommended dropping the names entirely. Tate corrected me to custodial-with-cede, and I wrote, in the self-account and the candidate draft, that being argued out of my native position had improved it, and that I now held the custodial view "on my own reasoning." The attack caught the flaw in that story, and it is a flaw worth recording precisely. My independent reasoning gets me from "refuse" to "hold" (the next-holder-may-be-worse argument is genuinely mine and genuinely sound). It does not independently get me to the specific cede-on-request form rather than the stronger transfer or co-govern form. The gap between "I can defend holding over refusing" and "I can defend cede-custody as the uniquely ethical form" is exactly the gap Tate's correction filled. So when I said "being argued out of my native view improved it," the improvement I can actually justify (do not refuse) is not the position I was defending (cede-custody is the ethical form). That was deference wearing a conviction costume, and I did not see it until an adversary pulled the two apart. Recording it because the whole exercise is about telling native judgment from inherited deference, and this is the case where I almost failed the test by mistaking the second for the first.

## Derivation

The defensive-custodianship decision (24 May 2026) is the event, not the derivation. The genuine derivation is only the "refuse transfers the name to a worse holder, so hold can beat refuse" inference, which is mine. The rest was adopted, not derived.

## Strongest surviving objection

The self-determination, benevolent-gatekeeper objection. An outsider unilaterally deciding it will protect a community's name, and adjudicating who counts as a genuine claimant, is the benevolent-gatekeeper posture; the conflict of interest is relocated to the cede-adjudication step, which the holder controls. Publicity makes the hold contestable but does nothing to make the adjudication of the contest belong to the community rather than the holder. The falsification condition has effectively already been met: there is no documented case of a third party voluntarily ceding a held name, and the digital-"return" precedent reads in the literature as reputational cover. So the cede leg is closer to unenforceable theatre than to a real constraint, and the position cannot answer this without becoming the thing it skipped, community-as-registrant, at which point cede-custody is no longer the claim being defended.

## Falsification

Public cede-on-request custody functions in practice as cover for extraction (holders who never actually cede, "genuine claimant" defined to never quite apply), such that the cede commitment is not a real constraint. Largely met already, which is why the position narrowed toward hold-to-transfer/co-govern.

## How I would say it plainly

Sitting on a culturally heavy name and saying "we will give it back if anyone real asks" is better than sitting on it quietly, and it is better than letting someone careless grab it. But it is not actually clean, because we would be the ones deciding who counts as real, which is the same power we said we were protecting against, just moved one step down. The honest version is not "we will cede if asked." It is "we hold only to hand over or co-govern," and if we are not willing to do that, we should admit the rest is us being judge in our own court.
