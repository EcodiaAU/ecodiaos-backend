## 3. PART A — RESONAVERDE REFERRING CLIENTS TO ECODIA

*(Mechanism unchanged from v2. Resonaverde refers clients seeking Ecodia Services to Ecodia. Ecodia pays commission.)*

### 3.1 Right of First Refusal

Where Resonaverde identifies a client or contact who is seeking AI, software or programming services, Resonaverde will first offer Ecodia a right of first refusal in respect of that opportunity, by notifying Ecodia in accordance with clause 3.2.

Ecodia will respond in writing within 5 business days indicating whether it accepts the referral. If Ecodia declines, does not respond within that period, or indicates that the scope is outside its capability, Resonaverde is free to refer that client to any other provider, and no Commission is payable under this Agreement in respect of that client.

Resonaverde is not otherwise restricted from referring clients to, or recommending, any other provider for services that fall outside the scope described in Appendix A.

### 3.2 Referral Process

To qualify as a "**Referred Client (A)**" under this Agreement, Resonaverde must submit a referral notice (email is sufficient) to Ecodia before Ecodia first contacts, or is contacted by, that client in relation to the Ecodia Services. The referral notice must identify the client, the primary contact, and the nature of services anticipated.

A client will only be treated as a Referred Client (A) once Ecodia has confirmed receipt of the referral notice in writing and has accepted the referral under clause 3.1. Clients who approach Ecodia independently, or who are introduced through channels other than a submitted and confirmed referral notice, are not Referred Clients (A).

### 3.3 Part A Commission

Ecodia will pay Resonaverde a commission (the "**Part A Commission**") on revenue actually received by Ecodia from Referred Clients (A), calculated on the GST-exclusive amount invoiced and paid, as follows:

- Upfront fees and initial development / project work: **15%**
- Ongoing support, retainer, maintenance, hosting, subscription or similar recurring services: **10%**

Part A Commission applies to all Ecodia Services invoiced to a Referred Client (A) within 24 months of Ecodia's first invoice to that client. After that 24-month period, no further Part A Commission is payable in respect of that client.

No Part A Commission is payable on: (a) reimbursed expenses or pass-through costs (e.g. third-party software licences, hosting, domain fees); (b) GST or other taxes; (c) amounts refunded, charged back, or written off as bad debts; or (d) revenue from clients who are not Referred Clients (A).

## 5. INVOICING, PAYMENT AND REPORTING

### 5.1 Monthly Reporting — Both Directions

Within 5 business days of the end of each calendar month:

(a) **Ecodia** will provide Resonaverde with a statement covering Part A Commissions — showing revenue received from each Referred Client (A) during the prior month, commissions accrued, any carried forward from prior months, and total Part A Commission payable for the month; and

(b) **Resonaverde** will provide Ecodia with a statement covering Part B Commissions — on the same basis for Referred Clients (B).

### 5.2 Invoicing and Payment — Both Directions

Following receipt of each monthly statement:

(a) Resonaverde will invoice Ecodia for the total Part A Commission payable. Ecodia will pay valid invoices within **14 days** of receipt.

(b) Ecodia will invoice Resonaverde for the total Part B Commission payable. Resonaverde will pay valid invoices within **14 days** of receipt.

Where the paying Party is registered for GST, the payee must issue a valid tax invoice and the paying Party will pay the applicable GST in addition to the Commission.

### 5.3 Audit Right — Both Directions

Each Party may, no more than once per calendar quarter, request a reasonable breakdown of invoices issued by the other Party to Referred Clients, for the purpose of verifying Commission calculations. The responding Party will reply within 10 business days.

If a material error greater than 5% of Commission payable is identified in the requesting Party's favour, the Party who made the error will bear the reasonable cost of the audit and promptly pay the shortfall; otherwise the cost is borne by the requesting Party.

## 7. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 7.1 Term

This Agreement commences on the date it is signed by both Parties and continues until terminated in accordance with this clause.

### 7.2 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement for any reason by giving the other Party at least **30 days'** written notice.

### 7.3 Termination for Breach or Insolvency

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if the other Party: (a) commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to remedy it within 14 days after receiving written notice requiring remedy; or (b) becomes insolvent, enters into administration, liquidation, or a similar process.

### 7.4 Effect of Termination on Commissions

On termination, both Part A and Part B Commissions are treated symmetrically as follows:

**Termination for convenience, or breach-termination by the non-breaching Party:** Commissions (both Part A and Part B) continue to be payable in respect of Referred Clients introduced before the termination date, on revenue received during a **tail period of 6 months** from the termination date, subject to the 24-month per-client cap in clauses 3.3 and 4.3.

**Termination by the breaching Party for their own breach, or termination triggered by insolvency:** The breaching or insolvent Party's outgoing Commissions cease on the termination date. Commissions owed TO the non-breaching Party are not affected.

Commissions accrued and unpaid as at the termination date remain payable in accordance with clause 5.
