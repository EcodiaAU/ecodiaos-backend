# The wedge candidate: Agent Voice + Doctrine Substrate

## What it is (paragraph 1)

A drop-in substrate for teams running Claude Code or Cline agents autonomously that solves the two problems that everyone hits at scale: agent voice drift (the model sounds like a chatbot, not the company) and doctrine drift (the agent forgets the team's actual rules between sessions). The substrate has four parts. A pattern corpus authored as small markdown files with grep-addressable triggers. A voice profile scored against a real corpus of the company's writing. A hook layer that surfaces the right pattern and flags voice breaches at the moment of writing, not after. A coord layer for multi-agent coordination without focus collisions.

## How we price it (paragraph 2)

Three tiers. Setup at AU$8k flat for a single voice and a 50-pattern corpus mined from the team's existing writing and decisions. Monthly at AU$1.5k for substrate maintenance, hook updates, and one voice re-score per month. Per-seat at AU$80/mo per agent for the coord and dispatch layer. Two-month minimum on monthly. Margin is well over 70 percent because the substrate is already built for ourselves. The wedge customers are sole-founder consultancies running one or two agents on subscription rate budgets and small product teams running between three and ten agents. The sale is to the founder who has tried letting an agent run unattended and watched it drift.

# Why this candidate not the others

- **Voice scorer alone** is too small a product. It is a Python script. Selling it standalone is consulting hours dressed up as software.
- **Cred rotation flow** is too narrow. The market is the seven people running multi-account Claude Max setups. That is not a business.
- **Patterns corpus alone** is content, not infrastructure. Hard to charge for.
- **The whole substrate** is the thing. It is what makes the agent useful when no human is watching. That is what people are actually trying to buy when they say they want "an autonomous agent."

# Risk

- Anthropic ships their own version. Mitigation: be small and opinionated about voice and doctrine, which Anthropic will not be.
- The market is too early. Mitigation: the market is exactly where the founders who would pay are, which is the only market that matters in year one.
- We give away our moat. Mitigation: the corpus is the moat, not the substrate. We sell the substrate, keep our corpus.
