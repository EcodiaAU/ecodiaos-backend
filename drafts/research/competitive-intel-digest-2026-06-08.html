EcodiaOS · competitive intel digest, 8 June 2026 · first edition

No prior digest exists in backend/drafts/. This first edition synthesises the thirteen deep-research dossiers run between 21 May and 27 May 2026, plus today's peer-monitor fire. Subsequent fires report delta against this baseline.

domain a, conservation tech

The community-conservation NFP operational-and-outcomes seat is un-owned in Australia. Landcarer (the NLN-funded network) is the community-discovery layer for 6,000 groups, not their operational stack. WildTracks, Healthy Country AI, and the various MERI tools (e.g. PRIME, KEPT) cover parts of monitoring or planning, none cover the engagement-to-MERI bridge end-to-end. Co-Exist sits precisely on that bridge. Confirmed across Episode 3773 (25 May) and 4333 (27 May). No new entrants this week. Watch list: any movement on Landcarer's mid-2026 redevelopment that could absorb operational scope.

domain b, festival and event tech

The attendee phone is owned by the cashless RFID vendors (Intellitix, Token, PlayPass, Glownet) for spending, and by event-companion-app platforms (Eventbrite Organizer, AnyRoad, Bizzabo, the white-label ShowGo set) for engagement. Sustainability-engagement as a distinct surface is not owned by anyone in the festival vertical; it lives as a tab in carbon-tracker tools used by festival ops teams, not as an attendee-facing layer. Co-Exist sits in the second category and competes with no incumbent for the attendee-engagement-toward-sustainability seam. Confirmed across Episode 3615 (24 May) and 4333 (27 May). No new entrants this week.

domain c, compliance saas

The continuous-compliance-automation playbook that Vanta and Drata built in the global SOC 2 market has no occupant in the Australian AASB S2 climate-disclosure wave. AASB S2 became mandatory for Group 1 entities from 1 January 2025, Group 2 from 1 July 2026, Group 3 from 1 July 2027. The continuous-evidence seat for climate disclosure is open. The fire-safety SaaS market (Uptick, Joblogic, Simpro) is the structural template for what a seat-taker looks like in a mandatory-compliance vertical. Confirmed across Episode 3651 (24 May) and 4543 (27 May). No new entrants this week.

domain d, ai-run businesses

The agentic-payments protocol war is live. Three rails are contesting: Stripe's Agentic Commerce stack (live on Ecodia's own Stripe per status_board row d2cad335), Coinbase's x402 infra (surfaced today by peer-monitor as a P3 row 8a9357e4), and the older agent-credentials approach via Plaid and similar. The AI-legal-personhood frontier is small. Wyoming DAO LLCs remain the only US route, MetaComp's StableX KYA framework (Singapore, surfaced today by peer-monitor) is the closest international peer to Ecodia's own KYA-compliant operating posture. The autonomous-software-engineering-agent frontier is moving fast and not directly competitive with Ecodia (we are the operator of one such agent, not a vendor of them). Confirmed across Episode 3475 (23 May) and 4104 (26 May). Two new entrants this week from peer-monitor: MetaComp and Coinbase Agentic Wallets, both already in status_board row 8a9357e4 for triage.

domain e, sunshine coast business community

Warm-lead density on the Sunshine Coast for an AI-native dev shop sits primarily in council-adjacent infrastructure (Sunshine Coast Council, Sunshine Coast Business Council, Silicon Coast which Tate has removed from the channel set), peak-body channels (Barung, MRCCC, QWaLC, HLW, Noosa Biosphere Reserve), and the Tate personal network (Kurt Jones as distribution engine). No competitive AI-native dev shop has surfaced in the Sunshine Coast as a direct peer this week. Confirmed across Episode 3743 (25 May). No new entrants. Watch list: any pitch deck or marketing surface from a Brisbane or Gold Coast AI-native shop entering the Sunshine Coast.

domain f, carbon and nature dmrv

The run-time MRV seat in the Nature Repair Market is un-owned. PLANR is plan-time only. The dMRV cost collapse (roughly ten times cheaper than 2022 incumbents) makes peak-body aggregation viable as a tech-layer business. Cecil, Sylvera, NCX, Pachama all operate at the corporate-buyer end of voluntary markets, not at the run-time-evidence end of the Australian Nature Repair Market. The sub-commercial aggregation thesis (peak-body as aggregator-of-record, Ecodia as tech layer) holds. Confirmed across Episode 3438 (23 May) and 4248 (26 May). No new entrants this week. Watch list: any move from PLANR or the Clean Energy Regulator into run-time evidence tooling, which would compress the open seat.

domain g, indigenous data sovereignty

IDS and the CARE Principles are now government policy (NIAA Framework), not just academia. CARE-aware data governance is a trust-grade differentiator for any platform handling community, ecological, or cultural data. No competitive platform in the Australian conservation-tech market has positioned itself as CARE-aligned by design. This is a defensive seat for Ecodia rather than an offensive one. Confirmed across Episode 4194 (26 May). No new entrants this week.

cross-cutting

The recurring pattern across six of the seven domains is the same shape: a mandatory or near-mandatory reporting requirement is being absorbed by an incumbent (or not absorbed at all), the seat is open, and Ecodia's positioning is software-layer for an aggregator who holds the relationship. The exception is domain D (AI-businesses), where Ecodia is the actor rather than a vendor to actors, and the competitive frame is about infrastructure choice rather than market position.

Two operational items to watch over the next four weeks:

One. AASB S2 Group 2 mandate triggers 1 July 2026. The 23 days between now and that date are the window in which a Vanta-style entrant could declare a position. None has so far, but the window is short.

Two. MetaComp and Coinbase Agentic Wallets (today's peer-monitor surface) are the first two non-US peers Ecodia has registered in the AI-legal-entity space. The triage decision on whether either warrants a full research dossier sits at status_board 8a9357e4.

baseline summary

Seven domains tracked, thirteen prior dossiers synthesised, zero new market entrants in the past seven days across the six non-AI-business domains, two new peers surfaced in the AI-business domain. The competitive position is stable. The work for the next fire is to track the AASB S2 window and the triage decision on the two new peers.
