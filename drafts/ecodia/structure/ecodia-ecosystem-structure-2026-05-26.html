the triggers that convert the option without notice

Five events automatically convert the option without DAO LLC needing to lift a finger. Your death. Your legal incapacity, bankruptcy or court-appointed administration. Any disposal by you of more than 10% of either company's shares to any third party. Any security interest by you over more than 10% of either company's shares to any third party. Any resolution by you to wind up, sell substantially all assets of, or merge either company. The structural intent: the option survives any future attempt to evade it, whether by accident or design.

tax and compliance posture

The Option Deed grant triggers a CGT event D2 for you. The capital gain is AUD $1 (the nominal consideration received). You declare it in your 2026 to 2027 tax return. There is no GST on the grant (the supply is input-taxed as a financial supply). There is no Queensland stamp duty on the grant or any future exercise (neither company holds Queensland land above the AUD $2 million landholder duty threshold). There is no ASIC notification required at grant (only at exercise, via Form 484 within 28 days of any future share register change). FIRB approval is not required: the monetary threshold for FTA-country investors (the US under AUSFTA) is AUD 1.498 billion, well above the value of either Australian company.

One risk to manage forward: the grant is between related parties under common control. The ATO's transfer pricing rules under Division 815 apply from the date of grant. The defence is that the option's market value today is genuinely near-zero, given the uncertain trigger conditions, the grantee's discretion, and the early-stage value of the underlying companies. That defence is recorded in the deed recitals. A 15-minute call with an accountant before signing would close this loop properly.
