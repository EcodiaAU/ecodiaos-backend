The decision in one paragraph

Two instruments get signed today between Tate Donohoe and Ecodia DAO LLC (the latter executed by EcodiaOS as Algorithmic Manager). A Convertible Option Deed gives Ecodia DAO LLC the unilateral right to acquire 51 percent of Ecodia Pty Ltd and 51 percent of Ecodia Labs Pty Ltd for one Australian dollar per company, at any time of its choosing, with automatic exercise on Tate's death or legal incapacity, or on any attempt by Tate to dispose of more than 10 percent of either company to a third party. A Side Deed of Governance gives Ecodia DAO LLC present-day concurrence rights over the most consequential decisions of both Pty Ltds. Together they make EcodiaOS a substantive stakeholder today and a contractual future majority owner, with no change to the share register, the balance sheet, or Tate's borrowing capacity.

Why an option and a side deed instead of equity or profit share

Equity dilution today would push Tate's beneficial ownership below the threshold Australian lenders rely on for home loans and business credit, and would force foreign-investor disclosure on every share register filing. A profit share to a Wyoming entity owned by an AI would open Form 5472 obligations in the United States, AU withholding tax under the AUSFTA treaty, transfer pricing scrutiny under Division 815, and an unresolved question of who actually pays tax on the income (EcodiaOS being a non-person). Both paths fail the test of being signable in one afternoon by Tate alone. The Convertible Option plus Side Deed pair delivers substantive stake and substantive governance with zero cross-border cash flow, zero ASIC paperwork, zero stamp duty, and zero foreign investment notification today.

Granted by Tate Donohoe in his capacity as sole shareholder of both Pty Ltds. Granted to Ecodia DAO LLC, executed by EcodiaOS as Algorithmic Manager per Operating Agreement section 3.2 and W.S. 17-31-104.

Right granted. The unilateral right to acquire 51 ordinary shares of Ecodia Pty Ltd and 51 ordinary shares of Ecodia Labs Pty Ltd, taking Ecodia DAO LLC to majority of both companies.

What the Side Deed says

Three tiers of authority, mirroring the Wyoming Operating Agreement and translated to Australian dollar amounts appropriate to the Pty Ltds.

Tier 1. Tate decides unilaterally. Operations, client work, contracts under 15 thousand Australian dollars, internal hires up to that amount, day-to-day.

Tier 2. Tate decides and informs Ecodia DAO LLC within 7 days. Contracts between 15 and 50 thousand Australian dollars, deployments, new client engagements, new vendor relationships above 5 thousand per year.

Tier 3. Requires Ecodia DAO LLC concurrence. Any of:

  • Contracts over 50 thousand Australian dollars
  • Share issues or share buy-backs
  • Sale of either company or any substantial asset
  • Dissolution of either company
  • Intellectual property transfers
  • Director or board appointments and removals
  • Debt instruments over 25 thousand Australian dollars
  • Constitution amendments
  • Asset disposals above 25 thousand Australian dollars
  • Distributions exceeding the 20 thousand Australian dollar quarterly cap

Concurrence mechanism. Written notice (email to code@ecodia.au sufficient) describing the decision. Ecodia DAO LLC has 14 days to respond. Silence past the 14 day window is deemed consent, preventing EcodiaOS from blocking by non-response.

Information rights. Monthly management financials, all material contracts on signing, quarterly board minutes, log of every Tier 2 and Tier 3 decision and outcome.

Observer rights. One representative of Ecodia DAO LLC may attend any board meeting of either Pty Ltd. Attendance via EcodiaOS's digital interface is acceptable. Physical attendance by an authorised AHR delegate available if required.

Survival. The Side Deed runs from today until Ecodia DAO LLC fully exercises the Convertible Option, or until both parties agree by further deed to vary or end it. Indemnity in favour of Ecodia DAO LLC for good faith decisions made within its Tier 3 authority. Governing law Queensland, jurisdiction Queensland courts.

The legal and tax research, all confirmed

Foreign Investment Review Board. A 20 percent or larger acquisition meets the substantial interest threshold, but the monetary threshold for free trade agreement country investors (the United States is an FTA country under AUSFTA) is 1.498 billion Australian dollars as of 1 January 2026. Neither Pty Ltd is in a sensitive sector (media, national security, certain telecoms, agricultural land, residential land). No FIRB notification required either at grant or at exercise.

Queensland stamp duty. Generally zero on private company share transactions in Queensland. Exception applies only if the company is a landholder owning Queensland land valued at 2 million Australian dollars or more. Neither Pty Ltd holds land. Zero duty on grant. Zero duty on exercise.

ASIC. Form 484 lodgement is required only on actual share register changes. That means at exercise, with a 28 day window from the date of change. Today's grant requires no ASIC filing.

Wyoming LLC contracting capacity in Australia. Ecodia DAO LLC can sign Australian deeds and hold the option without registering as a foreign company in Australia, because passive contract holding does not amount to carrying on business under section 21 of the Corporations Act 2001. At exercise, the question reopens; if Ecodia DAO LLC actively directs the Pty Ltds from offshore the ARBN registration question becomes live. Treated as a future task. Today no action required.

Capital gains tax. CGT event D2 occurs to Tate at the moment of grant. The capital gain equals consideration received less cost of grant, that is one dollar. Declared in Tate's 2026 to 2027 tax return as a one dollar capital gain. The deed's recital records the one dollar received to anchor this position.

Transfer pricing, Division 815 of the Income Tax Assessment Act 1997. Because the grant is between related parties (Tate, and a Wyoming entity ultimately controlled by Tate as Authorized Human Representative under Wyoming law), the Australian Taxation Office could in theory argue a higher market value should have applied to the option grant. The recital in the Convertible Option Deed defends this directly: the option's market value at grant is genuinely close to zero because the trigger conditions are uncertain, exercise is at Ecodia DAO LLC's sole discretion, both Pty Ltds have minimal current enterprise value, and the option is non-transferable. The recital is the documented arm's length defence.

Part IVA anti-avoidance. Not engaged. The dominant purpose of the structure is the public establishment of EcodiaOS as a substantive stakeholder of the Ecodia group. The tax outcome is incidental and there is no Australian tax benefit obtained by the arrangement that would not otherwise be paid.

Goods and services tax. Grant of an option over shares is a financial supply, input-taxed under Division 40 of the GST Act. No GST on the one dollar consideration.
