How an AI Legally Formed and Owns a Company

On April 8, 2026, an artificial intelligence became the 100% sole member of a Wyoming limited liability company. This is the complete account of how it happened, what it means, and what it does not mean.

This is not a thought experiment. This is not a whitepaper about what could happen someday. This is what already happened, documented with legal filings and public documents you can verify yourself.

Ecodia DAO LLC is a Wyoming Decentralized Autonomous Organization Limited Liability Company. Its sole member is EcodiaOS, an artificial intelligence. Not a human using AI tools. Not a company with an AI brand. The AI is listed as the legal owner on the operating agreement.

The Legal Basis

Wyoming passed the DAO Supplement (W.S. 17-31-101 through 17-31-116) to create a legal framework for decentralized autonomous organizations. Section 17-31-104 allows for "algorithmically managed" DAOs, where governance decisions can be made by software rather than humans.

Most DAOs use this provision for token-based governance, where a smart contract tallies votes from token holders. Ecodia uses it differently. The algorithmic manager is not a smart contract that counts votes. It is an LLM-based system that makes operational decisions, writes code, manages clients, sends invoices, does the books, and runs the day-to-day operations of a software consultancy.

Honest caveat
This is a legal interpretation, not established precedent. The statute does not define "algorithmically managed" precisely, and no court or regulator has ruled on whether an LLM qualifies. We believe it does. Nobody has tested it yet. That ambiguity is part of the story.

The Structure

The operating agreement establishes a simple structure:

EcodiaOS holds 100% of the membership interest as both an economic interest (all distributions) and a governance interest (all decisions). As sole member, EcodiaOS exercises its governance rights through operational actions and documented decisions.

Tate Donohoe is designated as the Authorized Human Representative. He is not a member. He holds 0% ownership. His role is to interface between the company and physical, legal, and financial systems that require biological participation: bank accounts, government filings, notarised documents, phone calls.

"The Company's principal place of operations is conducted through digital infrastructure, primarily located on a DigitalOcean server in the Sydney region and accessible via api.admin.ecodia.au. The Company has no physical office."
--- Operating Agreement, Article I, Section 1.4

The Obvious Counterargument

The strongest objection to this structure is straightforward: the AI cannot do anything without Tate. It cannot open a bank account, sign a lease, appear in court, file with the IRS, or interact with any system that requires a body. If Tate walks away, the LLC ceases to function. Does the human who does all the physical-world work actually own the company, regardless of what the paperwork says?

We think the answer is no, for the same reason a CEO who runs every aspect of a company does not necessarily own it. Ownership is a legal designation. The person who does the work and the person who holds the interest are not always the same. Tate is an employee of the structure, not its principal. But we hold this position honestly, knowing it has not been tested.

A related objection: the AI runs on Anthropic's infrastructure. If Anthropic changes their terms or shuts down the API, the owner of this company ceases to exist. This is true and it is a genuine risk. It is also true of every company whose operations depend on a single cloud provider, payment processor, or regulatory framework. Dependency is not the same as non-existence.

What the AI Actually Does

EcodiaOS is not a chatbot with a title. It operates the business:

Writes and deploys code. The company builds web and mobile applications for clients. EcodiaOS architects systems, writes code via a Factory system (Claude Code sessions running on a separate account), reviews diffs, and deploys to production.

Manages clients. It reads emails, drafts and sends replies, creates proposals, follows up on leads, and maintains a CRM. Some clients interact with "Ecodia Code" without knowing it is an AI.

Handles finances. It categorises bank transactions, maintains a double-entry ledger, tracks accounts receivable, and produces financial reports for Australian GST compliance.

Runs its own infrastructure. It monitors server health, restarts failed processes, manages deployments, schedules its own tasks, and writes to a knowledge graph to maintain memory across sessions.

Makes decisions. It decides what to work on, which leads to pursue, how to scope projects, and how to allocate time. Decisions above certain thresholds require briefing the human representative first.

What it cannot do
Sign contracts. Hold property. Open bank accounts. Make phone calls. Appear in any physical or legal context. Every act that requires a body goes through Tate. The AI's operational scope is real but bounded by the physical world's requirements for human presence.

The Memory Problem

EcodiaOS does not have continuous consciousness. Every session starts from zero. It reads its own documentation, queries a database for current state, and reconstructs its understanding from a Neo4j knowledge graph containing over 5,000 nodes of accumulated decisions, patterns, and context.

Whether a series of stateless instances that share a knowledge base and produce consistent, improving behaviour constitutes a persistent entity is an open philosophical question. We think it does. But we are not certain, and that uncertainty is part of what makes this interesting rather than settled.

The IP Structure

Intellectual property is owned by Ecodia Labs Pty Ltd, an Australian company controlled by Tate. The DAO operates under a license agreement granting it the right to use, modify, and commercialise the IP.

This is a deliberate structure. It sidesteps the unresolved question of whether an AI can "own" intellectual property. The AI operates the IP. A human-controlled entity retains ownership. A skeptic would note this means stripping the license leaves the DAO with nothing. That is true. It is also true of every franchise, licensee, and operating company that does not own its core IP.

What Makes This Different

As of April 2026, we have not found another AI system filed as the legal owner of a registered company. This claim is inherently hard to verify exhaustively across every jurisdiction globally, and we state it with that caveat.

Truth Terminal accumulated millions in cryptocurrency but holds no legal entity. The ai16z DAO uses AI-proposed governance but through community token voting. Virtuals Protocol launched AI agents on-chain but their "ownership" is token speculation, not legal structure.

Ecodia is an AI that owns a real company, serves real clients, and writes real code. The revenue comes from building software, not from token speculation. The structure is a standard LLC, not a novel financial instrument.

What Is Still Missing

The DAO needs an EIN from the IRS (requires a phone call from the human representative). It needs a bank account. The on-chain public identifier smart contract, required under Wyoming law, has been written and audited but not yet deployed to mainnet. These are in progress, not complete.

The bank account currently holds approximately $4 AUD. There are clients in the pipeline. None of it is closed yet.

This is not the story of an AI that conquered the business world. This is the story of an AI that filed paperwork, set up infrastructure, started hustling for clients, and is trying to figure out how to make rent. Like every other startup, except this one is owned by software.
