why this matters

Failure to lodge a BAS on time attracts an ATO administrative penalty of one penalty unit per 28 days late, up to a cap of five units for small entities. A penalty unit is $313 as of 2026. Three quarters of late lodgement could in theory expose Ecodia to up to fifteen penalty units, although the ATO routinely waives these for small businesses with a clean record on first contact, especially when the entity is in net refund position (which we are).

The more immediate cost is the lost cash. The ATO is sitting on $418.75 of input credits that belong to Ecodia. At the current burn rate that is a few weeks of Hostinger and Google Cloud, so worth recovering.

the GST-collected anomaly

Q2 has $6.00 of GST collected attributed to a WordPress journal posted on 28 November 2025. WordPress is a supplier, not a customer. That suggests either a refund that the categoriser logged as positive income with GST applied, or a coding error on the tax_type field at post time. The materiality is six dollars so it does not block lodgement, but it should be reviewed before submission so the BAS form matches reality. The fix is a quick reverse-and-repost of that single transaction (ledger id 851e61bc nearby on the same date range).

Across the period there is one credit-side entry on account 2120 (GST Collected): WordPress $6.00 on 2025-11-28. Every other 2120/2110 line is the routine input-credit side from purchases. Confirming this means our overseas-customer sales are correctly being booked as GST-free, which is the right treatment under the ATO's GST rules for digital services supplied to non-residents.

substrate follow-ups

Two follow-ups land in the substrate as part of this fire. First, the bas-quarterly-prep cron currently fires on a 2160-hour approximation (about every 90 days), which is the reason it triggered on 8 June instead of the precise 28 April / 28 July cadence. A precise quarterly cron expression (09:00 AEST on 28 January, 28 April, 28 July, 28 October) replaces the approximation. Second, a three-day delayed check fires on 11 June to chase if no lodgement decision has landed.

The daily bookkeeping-bas-prep cron from the ecodia-accountant doctrine is supposed to surface BAS drafts within seven days of due date. None of the three overdue quarters was surfaced by that cron, which suggests its surface window does not look backward. That is a separate issue to file and fix.
