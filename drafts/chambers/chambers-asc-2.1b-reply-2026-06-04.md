Thank you for the review. Chambers is a B2B SaaS platform built for chambers of commerce, the local member-based business associations that exist in towns and cities across Australia. Answers to your five questions:

1. Who are the users that will use the paid features and services in the app?

Members of individual chambers of commerce. Each chamber on the platform (for example Sunshine Coast Youth Chamber of Commerce, used as the demo tenant for review) is an independent member-based business association. Membership is a B2B relationship between the member's business and the chamber, established and paid for entirely outside the app via the chamber's own onboarding process, typically a signed membership agreement and an EFT invoice paid by the member's business.

2. Where can users purchase the features and services that can be accessed in the app?

Memberships and member-priced event tickets are purchased outside the app, through the chamber organisation directly. The typical paths are: contacting the chamber by phone or email, signing the chamber's printed or PDF membership agreement, paying the chamber's invoice by EFT or bank transfer, or registering and paying in person at chamber events. The app contains no purchase capability for these items, no "buy" button, no link to an external storefront for chamber memberships, and no signup flow that begins a purchase.

3. What specific types of previously purchased subscriptions can a user access in the app?

Chamber memberships purchased directly from the chamber organisation. These are annual or pro-rata B2B memberships invoiced by the chamber to the member's business (the SCYCC standard membership in the demo data is set at approximately AUD 330 per year, paid by EFT). They are not consumer subscriptions, not sold through any digital storefront, and not marketed inside the app.

4. What paid content, subscriptions, or features are unlocked within the app that do not use In-App Purchase?

Nothing is unlocked by any in-app payment. The app is the digital surface of an existing real-world membership. A signed-in member sees event listings (with member and non-member ticket prices set by the chamber), the member directory, committees and focus group threads, the chamber's newsletters, and their own dues status. All of these reflect the member's existing relationship with their chamber, which is owned and controlled by the chamber organisation outside the app. The chamber controls who is a member; the app reflects that state through the user's authenticated chamber account.

5. Can users purchase physical goods or services together with digital content in your app? If so, please describe how the physical and digital content are connected and why you bundle them together in a single purchase.

No in-app purchases occur. Chamber membership is a real-world B2B business relationship that includes both physical components (in-person networking events, member-only physical resources, voting rights at AGMs, advocacy representation with local government and council) and digital components (this app, newsletters, online committee threads). These are inherently part of the same membership because chamber membership is the underlying B2B service the member buys from the chamber; the app is one of several channels through which the chamber delivers value to that membership. The app does not bundle, market, or sell anything itself; it shows the member their existing rights.

For App Store classification, Chambers fits the Enterprise / B2B Services pattern under Guideline 3.1.3. The product is sold by Ecodia to chambers of commerce as the operating customer, and each chamber then makes the app available to its existing membership base as one digital channel into an existing real-world B2B relationship.

Demo credentials provided in App Review Information (apple@ecodia.au / appleecodia) sign in to the Sunshine Coast Youth Chamber of Commerce as a member with the President role, so all admin surfaces are visible.
