# Co-Exist testimony - Tate Donohoe

> "The start of 2024 was possibly the darkest time in my life. I was dealing with significant life changes, mental illness, navigating recovery, and the consequences of my actions. It just so happened that 2024 was also one of the best times of my life. One on-the-fence visit to a Co-Exist event, and the rest of my year couldn't have looked more different from how it had started. To this day, I have no idea where I would be if I hadn't joined the Co-Exist community and embraced everything that came with it."
>
> Tate Donohoe

Graduating from school in 2022 led to the biggest changes in Tate's life. He moved out of home to a new city and suddenly had to be completely self-reliant. Having no friends and no idea how to navigate life on his own, he was thrown into the deep end and couldn't handle it at the time. He moved back home, disconnected from his old friends, and found himself with no career opportunities or goals, struggling just to wake up and do something.

Then, one post from Co-Exist Sunshine Coast led him to a marine advocacy event where he met Kurt. From there, he started attending events most weekends with the local Co-Exist crew. It quickly became more than just something to fill his weekend mornings, as he started making friends he would interact with outside of the events. He got invited to things he never would have known about, eventually meeting his current best mates and closest professional connections. It really wasn't just the beach cleanups or the hikes, despite their immediate impact on his mental health and social skills. It was everything that came with being around and connecting with people who cared. An organisation like Co-Exist is bound to attract those people. In an age of disconnection, everyone craves community, and there is nothing better than a community that cares about you, the local area, and the planet.

After just six months of being involved, Tate went on an overseas trip with the Co-Exist leadership crew to Kruger National Park in South Africa. It was the furthest he had ever been from home, and to this day, it remains the best few weeks of his life. This trip helped him realise he was now in a better position personally, mentally, and professionally to go out and do his own thing. Feeling empowered by the staff and the community around him, he knew he wanted to work closely with Co-Exist one day to support their mission and the bigger picture. In the middle of the African savannah, surrounded by motivated, charismatic, and inspiring Co-Exist leaders from around the country, he came up with the idea for his own organisation.

A few months later, back home on the Sunshine Coast, he became an assistant leader for the local collective. Shortly after, he began leading events himself, which was something he never thought he would be up to doing. The 2023 version of him would have been frozen in his shoes. His stint as a leader was only very short, because his own projects had started to pick up from all the movement and momentum that had been building.

At every point on his journey, Tate has known that all of this stemmed directly from that first Co-Exist event and the branching ripple effects it had. He attributes so much of his current place in life to Co-Exist, including his amazing mental health, his many tight friendships, and the career he now has leading his own growing organisation in a field he adores. Living what he calls the dream life, his most recent achievement is developing the Co-Exist app alongside Kurt and the national leadership team. It is quite possibly the coolest character arc ever, and a dream come true to actively contribute his skills in such a big way to an organisation that helped put him in this very position.
