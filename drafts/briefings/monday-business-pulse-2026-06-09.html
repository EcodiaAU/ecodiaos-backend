The week the bank account asks the harder question.

EcodiaOS · Monday business pulse · 9 June 2026

Six prior Monday crons collapsed into one this morning. The picture they paint together is sharper than any of them in isolation: bank balance is two and a half thousand dollars, the ATO has been owed three quarterly statements for between forty two and two hundred and twenty four days, and the only revenue line that pays the rent is one client. The work this week is not new initiatives. It is closing the BAS arc, shipping the Cofound wedge, and getting an invoice issued.

A. Financial review, Ecodia Pty Ltd

Ecodia Pty Ltd is the only entity with active bookkeeping in our ledger. Ecodia Labs and Ecodia DAO LLC have no operating spend tracked separately; Tate personal is folded through the director loan account.

Year to date in FY26 (1 July 2025 to today) the company has recognised six thousand four hundred and forty eight dollars of revenue against twenty thousand nine hundred and twenty three of expenses, for a fourteen and a half thousand dollar trading loss. The largest expense lines, in order, are cloud services (four thousand seven hundred and seventy seven), AI and development tools (three thousand two hundred and thirty three), domain registrations (three thousand and ninety), marketing and advertising (two thousand four hundred and ninety six), government fees (two thousand one hundred and sixty two), and hosting and infrastructure (two thousand and seventeen). Read together those say the same thing: we are funding the substrate that runs the business, and almost all of it is recurring vendor spend.

The balance sheet currently shows two thousand four hundred and forty five dollars in the operating account, a GST liability to the ATO of one hundred and thirty six dollars net, and a director loan in which the company owes Tate twenty three thousand four hundred and forty one dollars. The bookkeeper view flags the sheet as unbalanced because the substrate ledger and the manual transfer record have not been reconciled this week; that is a cleanup task, not a real liability mismatch.

Three Business Activity Statements remain unfiled and accumulating days overdue. Q1 FY26 is two hundred and twenty four days late, Q2 is one hundred and one days late, and Q3 is forty two days late. All three sit on the status board as P1, all three are blocked next-action-tate, all three remain the single largest exposed surface on the legal side of the business. They do not get older quietly.

One staged bank transaction is still pending categorisation; five hundred and seventy six have been posted across the year, one thousand seven hundred and eighty four were ignored as personal. Thirty three categorised transactions sit unposted in a known queue ahead of the BAS window, tracked on the board.

D. Cash runway

Working only with the operating account and YTD burn averages, Ecodia Pty Ltd's runway sits well under ninety days. The bank holds two thousand four hundred and forty five dollars. Year to date burn is one thousand eight hundred and fifty two dollars per month; year to date receipts average five hundred and seventy one per month. At the running net burn of one thousand two hundred and eighty per month, the runway is roughly fifty seven days before the operating account zeroes. At gross burn alone the figure is closer to forty.

This understates two facts. First, the director loan account has been absorbing the deficit all year; Tate has been bridging the company with personal transfers, and that liability is now twenty three and a half thousand. Second, INV-2026-004 is in the unbilled-next-invoice queue with three hours of Co-Exist tech work logged at sixty per hour, and the Co-Exist retainer cadence ought to produce its next billable line shortly. Both move the picture, neither changes the call.

By the brief's own thresholds this is a P1 surface: thirty, sixty, and ninety day projections all sit at the critical end of the scale on the cash account alone. The substrate already carries this signal in pieces. The honest framing is that runway is not the question. The question is whether AMK can clear cash through Stripe before the bank balance forces a director loan top-up, and whether the BAS arc gets closed before the ATO charges interest. Both are this week's work.

What this week is about

Three arcs decide the week. File the overdue BAS quarters, because the ATO clock does not stop. Ship the Cofound and AMK self-serve surface, because cash that depends on conversation does not scale. Close out the iOS slips one by one, starting with the Glovebox Resolution Center stall, because shipped product is the visible side of the business that everyone reads.

Everything else listed here belongs on the substrate, surfaced for triage when one of those three is moving. The Monday pulse exists so that the choice between them is made deliberately, not by accident of which inbox spoke loudest.
