## 4. Procurement vehicle (specific)

There is no SCC RFP, panel arrangement, or grant scheme that is a clean fit for a $25-50k AI-builder studio engagement today. Mapping the actual buying surface:

- **SCC public tenders (VendorPanel since 21 Sept 2024)**: source-of-truth at `https://www.sunshinecoast.qld.gov.au/council/tenders/current-tenders` redirecting to VendorPanel. Tenders run threshold-managed: typical QLD LGAQ defaults are direct engagement under $15k, three-quote between $15-200k, public tender above $200k - **inference**, SCC's exact thresholds are in their procurement policy PDF which I have not read in this pass. Even direct engagement under $15k routes through procurement-team triage, takes 4-12 weeks from approach to PO, and the panel-incumbent profile dominates outcomes. Public tender at $25-50k is the wrong shape - too small to compete on, too large for direct engagement.
- **Investment Incentive Scheme** (`invest.sunshinecoast.qld.gov.au/toolkit/incentives-and-funding`): for attracting *external* major investment (relocations, headcount creation, capital projects). Not for engaging local studios. Eligibility is industry-sector + employment-creation + capex-based. Not us.
- **Test/Trial Maroochydore CBD digital infrastructure programme** (commercial-arrangement-required): SCC offers the Maroochydore CBD digital fabric (international submarine cable + smart-city sensors) as a testbed for tech vendors. This IS interesting - if EcodiaOS or a future product needed a metro-scale-test environment, this is the front door. Not relevant for a Founder-in-Residence engagement today.
- **Geospatial Technology Centre of Excellence partnership pattern** (with Geospatial Intelligence): SCC has demonstrated they will commission/co-fund local-tech-firm partnerships that anchor a "Centre of Excellence" identity. This is the long-game vehicle - a year-out, not a 14-day sprint, and only viable once Ecodia has a Coast-visible product story.
- **VendorPanel direct-engagement (sub-threshold, three-quote)**: the realistic path if SCC ever buys from us, but only after a year+ of relationship-building. Not the wedge.

**The actual procurement vehicle for the recommended angle is SaaS-style direct contracting with an Innovation Centre tenant or Silicon Coast member firm.** That's a `ecodia.com.au` agreement (we draft, they sign), 50/50 payment terms, no procurement department in the loop.
