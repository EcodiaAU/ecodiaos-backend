## Problem / Gap

**No dedicated mobile app.** Confirmed via web and App Store search - zero results for Horizon Festival apps on iOS or Android.

**No schedule builder.** The website (Netlify + Kontent.ai headless CMS, modern architecture) has solid event filtering across:
- 13 venues
- 10 accessibility categories (wheelchair, hearing assistance, low vision, low sensory, etc.)
- 13 content categories (Comedy, Family, Film, First Nations, Food, Free, Interactive, LGBTQIA+, Music, Performance, Technology, Visual Art, Words + Ideas)
- Day-by-day date filtering
- On Sale / Sold Out inventory status

But zero personalisation, no "My Schedule", no push notifications, no mobile-native experience.

**Ticketing platform:** Mobtix (separate platform, no integration into a unified experience).

**The gap is clear:** sophisticated content filtering exists on the website, but attendees managing a 10-day festival across 13 venues have no way to build a personal program and carry it on their phone. The accessibility filtering - which signals strong values - has no mobile-native equivalent.

---

## What We Solve

A bespoke Horizon Festival app for 2027:
- Personal schedule builder (save events, daily agenda view)
- Push notifications (reminders, last-minute changes, sold-out alerts)
- Venue maps (13 venues, real-time navigation)
- Artist/event pages (rich content, linked to headless CMS)
- Accessibility filter persistence (saved preferences across sessions)
- Community features (optional - social check-ins, shared schedules)
- Built to Horizon's visual identity

This directly embodies Bec Martin's philosophy: lowering friction for community members, especially those navigating complex accessibility needs across a multi-venue 10-day programme.
