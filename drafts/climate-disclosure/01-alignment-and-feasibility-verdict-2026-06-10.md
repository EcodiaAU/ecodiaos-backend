## Corrections to the brief, loudly (per the brief's own instruction)

1. The demand clock was mis-aimed. 1 July 2026 is not a filing deadline three weeks out. Group 2 means an entity's FIRST annual reporting period commencing between 1 July 2026 and 30 June 2027 (Corporations Act s 1707B); a June balancer's first sustainability report lodges by 30 September 2027 (disclosing entities) or 31 October 2027 (others) under s 319(3) and ASIC RG 280 Table 2. The true urgency is better: the first reportable YEAR starts in three weeks for June balancers, and evidence not captured from day one is reconstructed in 2028 under an auditor's scepticism. Continuous evidence is the one product whose pitch IS the corrected deadline.
2. AASB S1 is voluntary in Australia. The obligation is AASB S2 plus the Corporations Act overlays (ss 296A to 296D), under Schedule 4 of the Treasury Laws Amendment (Financial Market Infrastructure and Other Measures) Act 2024, No. 87 of 2024. The brief's "AASB S2 and AASB S1" framing would have been a public error.
3. Year-one limited assurance for Group 2 is broader than governance plus Scope 1 and 2: ASSA 5010 para 10(a) adds the strategy disclosures identifying climate-related risks and opportunities (AASB S2 paras 9(a), 10(a), 10(b)). Scenario analysis, transition plans, risk management and metrics/targets carry no assurance in year one, move to limited assurance in years two and three, and everything reaches reasonable assurance from Group 2's fourth year (FYs commencing on or after 1 July 2029, ahead of the s 301A statutory floor of 1 July 2030).
4. The sustainability report auditor need not be the financial report auditor (ASIC FAQs; ss 301A, 1707E, 1707F): any Corporations Act auditor with registered company auditors leading. Only RSEs are locked to one auditor. This widens the partner geometry: a firm can hold the sustainability assurance seat at an entity it does not financially audit, and that firm still cannot prepare what it assures.
5. The measurement default is the GHG Protocol Corporate Standard (AASB S2 para 29(a)(ii)); the AASB expressly rejected NGER-as-default (BC37). NGER reporters may elect NGER Determination methods per facility under the jurisdictional relief, hybrid allowed; AASB S2025-1 (Dec 2025) extends the relief family. Our calc engine records a per-facility method election for exactly this reason.
6. Modified liability: protected statements (Scope 3, scenario analysis, transition plans) in reports for FYs commencing 1 Jan 2025 to 31 Dec 2027 are shielded from private actions; ASIC and criminal actions remain. That window covers a Group 2 entity's first report and is a selling point for acting now. The broader forward-looking-statement shield expired with FYs commencing in 2025 and is irrelevant to Group 2.
7. First-year reliefs are real and shape the SoW: no comparatives (C3), no Scope 3 (C4(b)) in year one; Scope 3 arrives in year two together with limited assurance over it. The directors' transitional declaration ("reasonable steps", s 1707C(2)) applies through FYs commencing 31 Dec 2027.
8. The buyer assumption repeated the wrong-audience error. The 4 May 2026 Strategic_Direction node records 30+ days of zero replies pitching a cohort Ecodia had no distribution into. Direct-to-CFO is that pattern one tier up. The GTM (07) inverts to partner-firm-first, the same infrastructure-under-the-relationship-owner shape [[carbon-mrv-wedge-peak-body-sub-commercial]] codifies.

## Question 2: can we handle it?

The regulated map, each line verified against primary sources in the research lane, none of it ours to perform:

1. Statutory assurance of sustainability reports: only a Corporations Act auditor (registered company auditors leading), under ASSA 5000/5010. We never sign, never conclude, never use the words assurance, audit, review, or any cognate, about our own deliverables. The contract says so.
2. NGER scheme audits: only CER-registered greenhouse and energy auditors (NGER Act Part 6). We may prepare NGER data; we never audit it.
3. Financial product advice or dealing in ACCUs, SMCs, EIEUs: AFS licensees only (Corporations Act Ch 7; ASIC INFO 156, RG 236). We provide factual emissions data; we never recommend acquiring, holding or disposing of units. Offset-strategy content stays factual and educational.
4. Tax agent services (TASA 2009 s 90-5): registered tax agents. Our data feeds their adviser; we do not opine on tax treatment.
5. Legal advice: the entity's lawyers. Boundary and contract questions route there by name in the runbook.

The structural advantage inside that map: APES 110 independence means the assurer cannot prepare what it assures. Every Group 2 entity therefore needs a preparation layer that is NOT its auditor, and the better that layer's lineage, the cheaper the assurance. The boundary that forbids us from assurance is the same boundary that guarantees the seat we are taking exists. This is the Cofound UPL pattern transposed: the formation industry runs unlicensed on self-help rails because judgement stays with the licensed party; disclosure preparation runs unlicensed on evidence rails because opinion stays with the auditor and the directors.

What handling it requires that we do not have today: professional indemnity insurance quoted for a disclosure-preparation technology service (pre-first-signature, Tate decision; market sweep 2026-06-10 at `pi-insurance-quotes-2026-06-10.md` corrects the original "low thousands" guess: single low thousands holds only at $1M to $2M limits without affirmative AI cover; $5M and $10M with the AI delivery model honestly disclosed runs $8k to $25k and $15k to $40k+ respectively, and an AI exclusion is the default market outcome that must be actively resisted in placement); one-time legal review of the SoW template (per-template-once, the Cofound pattern, gated behind the first serious prospect); and the W1 to W9 build (5 to 7 dispatched worker-days, zero external spend). Greenwashing law (ASIC Act s 12DA) binds our public artifacts regardless of licensing; the no-overclaiming rule in every Phase 5 to 6 surface is a legal control, not a style preference.

## The strongest case against, steelmanned

A skeptical reader should push here, so the pushing is done in advance. First: nobody buys compliance from a no-name; true, which is why the GTM measures partner conversations and ships a verifiable sample pack instead of promises, and why Gate 3 kills the line if no signature arrives by 31 December 2026. Second: preparation work can still attract liability if a disclosure we drafted misleads; the modified-liability shield covers the ENTITY's protected statements and does nothing for a third-party preparer, and ASIC's greenwashing appetite is proven at scale (Mercer $11.3M, 24-173MR; Vanguard $12.9M record, 24-213MR). Our controls: the contract (entity owns every statement, drafts grounded in their evidence, exclusions and no-reliance language in plain words), the schema-level grounding gate, capped contractual liability, and PI insurance quoted before the first signature. The completeness critic in wf_a0b7562b-a49 also flagged the terminology perimeter: a deliverable styled "readiness review" or "verification" could be construed as a voluntary assurance engagement under ASSA 5000 (which covers voluntary engagements too), so the SoW carries a banned-terms list and the whole perimeter goes to the template legal review. Third: a Big-4 or a software vendor could copy this; the Big-4 cannot publish reproducible delivery without cannibalising their hours and cannot prep what they assure, and the software vendors sell tools while the entity still does the work; the genuinely contested seat is the mid-tier firms' back office, which is why we offer to BE that back office rather than fight it. Fourth: auditors may distrust AI-prepared figures; the disclosed numbers come from a deterministic engine with golden tests, model judgement touches only classification and prose, and every figure recomputes from hashed sources, which is more than can be said for the year-one consultant models practitioners already report as unreproducible. Fifth: the regime might soften politically; the 2026-27 Budget proposals moved large-proprietary and Group 3 boundaries and left Group 2 dates and thresholds untouched (verified in the buyers lane), and the standards-watch cron exists precisely to catch drift early.
