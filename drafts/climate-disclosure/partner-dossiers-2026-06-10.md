# Partner-firm dossiers for Channel A (climate-disclosure GTM)

The brief from `07-gtm-and-demand-clock-2026-06-10.md` re-sequences Channel A: boutiques first (Pangolin already received a batch-1 email on 2026-06-10), mid-tier accounting firms move to October 2026, and every target must pass an existing-platform-alliance check before any first-contact send. This dossier is the output of that check, the named-lead identification, the conflict review and the firm-specific hook drafting for each candidate.

The offer the firm sees is the partner-channel variant of the SoW per `02-productised-sow-template-2026-06-10.md` as red-team-amended: a flat annual platform licence at AUD 40-60k with unlimited engagements plus a per-engagement onboarding fee the firm marks up, never pitched as deleting their hours (APES GN 30 supervision means their review layer is un-removable), always disclosed-platform per APES 305 (subprocessor disclosure plus the security one-pager and data-flow diagram the firm hands its client), never silent white-label. The hook references the year-two Scope 3 clock (the regime's biggest cost, cannot be retrofitted) and the disclosed-platform posture (continuity through client-owned Supabase + escrowed monthly exports), never the refutable "we delete your hours" or "Scope 1/2 reconstruction" lines.

## Headline finding: the mid-tier seat is already occupied

The platform-alliance scan returned a structural result that reshapes the channel sequencing. Of the seven mid-tier accounting firms named in the original 07-gtm Channel A list, six have a verifiable carbon-accounting platform alliance (Sumday, Avarni, Workiva, Trace) already, and the seventh (William Buck) appears as a user-firm in news reporting on Sumday's customer base even though William Buck's own page does not advertise the relationship. Sumday alone has gathered BDO, Pitcher Partners, William Buck (named in news), Grant Thornton (national arrangement), Moore Australia (VIC/TAS), PKF, Baker Tilly and Forvis Mazars under one directory; Avarni explicitly names BDO Australia as a partner; Bentleys Queensland is a published Trace partner; Grant Thornton and RSM both have Workiva relationships (RSM Workiva-certified at US/global level, Grant Thornton named as a Workiva partner). The implication is plain: pitching any of these firms in October 2026 is pitching an occupied seat unless our offer reframes around something the incumbent platform cannot do (client-owned Supabase, evidence hash-chain, mapped clause-by-clause draft statements with register-row citations). That reframing is real and shippable, but it changes the message and lowers the win probability versus the open boutique surface.

Pangolin Associates is the cleanest pure-play boutique with no platform alliance discovered. Northmore Gordon (Pangolin's energy-side strategic partner) is also clean and is a natural co-pitched candidate. NettZero appears to operate without a publicly disclosed third-party platform alliance. Ndevr Environmental (now branded Anthesis Australia after the 2024 merger) operates its own first-party tool "Anthesis Intelligence" and is best read as a sophisticated competitor with deep practice depth, not a partner candidate. Edge Impact is a published technology partner of Greener (joint compliance platform launch) and is the same shape: not a partner candidate without our offer reframing as additive to Greener.

The recommended action sequence in light of this is: deepen the Pangolin pursuit (batch-1 email already sent), open Northmore Gordon as a co-pitch, scope NettZero, and demote every mid-tier in this dossier to a conditional pitch contingent on the incumbent-platform-reframing message working in test conversations with the boutiques first.
