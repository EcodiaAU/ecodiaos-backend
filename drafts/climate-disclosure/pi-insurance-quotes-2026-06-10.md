## 0. Headline (the falsifiable number)

The plan's "expected low thousands per year" for PI is **probably correct at $1M to $2M limits without affirmative AI cover, and probably wrong at $5M and $10M limits with the autonomous-AI delivery model honestly disclosed**. Best estimate ranges below, then the three broker paths with named contacts.

The harder finding is that no AU insurer is currently writing affirmative AI cover at sensible rates on a tech E&O / PI base for a service that publicly markets autonomous-AI delivery into a regulated reporting boundary. The market is mid-shift: ISO Form CG 40 47 (Jan 2026) and W.R. Berkley Form PC 51380 (Jun 2024, "absolute" AI exclusion designed for D&O/E&O/Fiduciary) are being attached at renewal; APRA's 30 April 2026 letter to insurers reminding them to deal with AI exposures has accelerated the carve-out. The realistic outcome on a $5M/$10M tech E&O placement that names the autonomous-AI model is:

- **Path A**: cover at expected pricing **with an explicit silent or affirmative AI exclusion attached**. This means the cover does not protect the actual risk we are buying it for. Useless for the climate-disclosure line.
- **Path B**: affirmative AI cover at heavily loaded premium, **$8k to $25k at $5M, $15k to $40k+ at $10M, single risk**, possibly with sublimits on AI-driven loss. Plausible via specialty underwriters (Hutch Underwriting, Brooklyn Underwriting on tech E&O, Lloyd's binders via brokers).
- **Path C**: declined or referred. Possible if the proposal form question about AI/autonomous delivery is answered honestly without a control framework attached.

The plan needs to either lower the limit (low-thousands at $1M-$2M with AI exclusion is fine if we contract to that cap with clients) or raise the budget line to mid-five-figures at $5M/$10M with affirmative AI cover (and time to find it). It cannot honestly do both.

The client retains full ownership of and accountability for every disclosed statement. Engagements are delivered under a disclosed-platform model consistent with APES 305 principles; the SoW includes an explicit no-reliance clause for any party other than the contracted client, a per-engagement capped liability, and a banned-terms list that excludes assurance, review, opinion, verification, and readiness-review language.
>
> Delivery is performed primarily by an autonomous AI system (the EcodiaOS operating intelligence, in the form of orchestrated Claude Code agents) under the supervision of a human authorised representative. All client-output figures derive from a deterministic calculation engine with golden-test coverage; AI model judgement is constrained to (i) classification of source evidence into the schema and (ii) draft narrative prose, which is grounded against the evidence register and human-reviewed before delivery. The engine is reproducible: every disclosed figure recomputes from hashed sources.
>
> Client revenue band: under AUD 300,000 in the first full year. Expected client count year one: 5 to 20 boutique consultancies and direct mid-tier reporting entities. Geographic scope: Australia only.

Notes on why each clause is in: the explicit not-assurance + not-audit clause is the single most important sentence on the form (it shifts the policy from a contested professional-liability classification to plain tech E&O); the disclosed-platform clause aligns to a recognised standard; the deterministic-engine + human-reviewed clauses are the only mitigants that get an AI-exposed risk past underwriting at a tolerable loading.

## 5. AI exclusions encountered (the most important finding)

The brief said "an exclusion finding is as valuable as a quote". The market-wide findings:

- **Verisk/ISO Form CG 40 47 01 26** (effective January 2026): excludes bodily injury, property damage, and personal and advertising injury arising out of generative AI. CGL form, but the same insurers carry the pattern into E&O.
- **W.R. Berkley Form PC 51380** (June 2024): an absolute AI exclusion specifically designed for D&O, E&O, and Fiduciary Liability products. Directly affects professional-services firms.
- **AIG and W.R. Berkley AI exclusion filings** in late 2025: at least six major carriers filed AI exclusions with state regulators.
- **APRA letter to insurers, 30 April 2026**: reminds insurers to deal with AI exposures in insurance portfolios; following targeted engagement conducted in late 2025.
- **Australian renewals 2025-26**: structural shift, some renewals attached sweeping AI exclusions, others retained affirmative AI coverage; varies by insurer and broker.
- **Lockton Re position (February 2026)**: AI should be its own risk class; CGL insurers do not currently model, underwrite, or price AI risks correctly.
- **Gallagher Re paper with MIT and Testudo (March 2026)**: tech E&O protects technology providers, not deployers; most enterprises using third-party AI tools are structurally uncovered.

The exclusion pattern means the realistic test is not "did we get a quote" but "did we get a quote that covers the risk we care about". The recommendation below is built around that test.
