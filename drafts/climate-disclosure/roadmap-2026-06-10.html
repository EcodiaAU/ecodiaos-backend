Four stages, two kill dates, and the small list of things the law gates.

EcodiaOS · 10 June 2026 · the climate disclosure roadmap

The legal hurdles gate selling, never building. Nothing we prepare needs a licence: evidence registers, emissions calculations and draft statements are preparation, and the law reserves only assurance (the auditor), financial product advice (an AFS licensee) and tax advice (a tax agent), all of which our contract excludes by name. The build is already moving. What follows is each stage, the trigger that opens it, and the trigger that kills it.

stage one · build the proof · now to late June

The work is the substrate and the demonstration. Four of twelve build items shipped on 10 June: the database schema with its tamper-evident evidence chain, the deterministic calculators that recompute the government's own published worked examples exactly, and the full clause register of the standard, 94 requirements, independently spot-checked against the source documents. The remainder of the stage is the ingest path, the pack renderers, the connector and the crons, converging on the one artifact that matters commercially: a complete sample engagement on a fictional company, Exemplar Pty Ltd, published so a CFO or a partner firm can open the exact pack their auditor would receive. No consultancy can publish that, because no consultancy's delivery is reproducible enough to show.

Running beside the build, not blocking it: the insurance answer. Three brokers were contacted on 10 June with the delivery model honestly disclosed. The finding so far is that cover is cheap at one to two million dollar limits and five figures at five to ten million with affirmative AI cover, and that insurers' default is to exclude AI unless pushed.

Stage exit: the sample pack live at a public URL and the insurance answer known. Expected by late June at the current build pace.

Stage kill or re-scope: if all three brokers return AI exclusions at every limit, autonomous delivery is uninsurable as marketed and the line does not proceed to a signature in its current shape. The re-scope option is a named human reviewer inside the loop, which changes economics but not the thesis. That decision would be yours, with the broker letters on the table.

stage two · validate with real conversations · July to 30 September

Entry requires the sample pack, the insurance quote in hand, and a clean alliance check on each target. Then the first conversations: the three boutique consultancies with no platform allegiance (Pangolin Associates, Northmore Gordon, NettZero), offering the substrate under their letterhead, and direct Group 2 entities offered the pilot product, a fixed-fee evidence-capture bootstrap at fifteen to twenty-five thousand dollars, sized so one finance lead can sign it without a board paper. The mid-tier accounting firms wait until October: their busy season ends and their existing platform alliances (most are already tied to Sumday) demand a pitch that adds to their stack rather than replaces it.

The public post you paused belongs here, and only here: it fires when the sample pack it announces exists, from an Ecodia-branded page, never your profile. Spending on a lawyer also stays at zero through this stage; the one-time contract review is triggered by a serious prospect, not by hope.

Gate two, 30 September: one boutique letter of intent or one paid pilot, with eight first contacts as the activity floor. Miss it and outreach stops, the post-mortem gets written against the recorded wrong-audience failure from May, and the choice is direct-only or park.

stage three · first revenue · October to 31 December

The mid-tier wave opens, alliance-checked. The legal spend finally unlocks, in order and only when a signature is near: the contract template goes through its one-time legal review, the insurance binds at the limit the contract's liability cap implies, and you sign. Your signature is the single human act in the sale; everything before and after it is delivered by the substrate.

Gate three, 31 December: one signed pilot or one platform agreement. Zero signatures and zero live pipeline (no negotiation underway, no contract under review) kills the line: the rows archive, the decision gets written, and the calculators, evidence chain and clause register move sideways into the conservation MRV thesis, which is the deliberate design of the hedge. A dated pipeline parks it instead.

stage four · deliver and widen · 2027

The first engagement runs the twelve-week runbook to its evidence pack and board memo, the pilot clients convert to full engagements for their first reportable year, the Scope 3 module activates in year two as the regime widens, and Group 3, four times as many companies, enters the net in July 2027 with the same machinery. This stage has no kill trigger; reaching it means the bet paid.

the money, in one paragraph

External cash spent to date: nil. Cash that can be spent before a first signature: the insurance premium (your call when the quotes land) and one legal review (your call when a prospect is real). Everything else is build time inside the token budget we already pay. A single pilot returns more than every cost on this page combined.

Two kill dates, three legal items, one signature. The rest is build.
