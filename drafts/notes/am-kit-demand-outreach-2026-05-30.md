I will not fabricate a list of thirty specific founders from memory. That produces plausible-looking names that may not exist or may be wrong, which is worse than no list. Instead this doc gives the real channels where this audience lives, an outreach message ready to send, and a sourcing method to gather genuine names. ## Who the buyer actually is

The buyer is a founder building an autonomous AI agent that does real work and wants the company structure to match. The pain is narrow and real for a small group: they want the agent to be the operating layer of a registered company, and the normal LLC mental model puts a human in the manager's chair. They have usually heard of Wyoming DAO LLCs or asked whether an AI can run a company. They are technical, they live online, and they value a working demo over a brochure.

This is a small, specific group. The whole point of the demand test is to find out whether it is large enough to matter, before spending on legal and build.

## Channels where they actually are

Ranked by fit, not size.

- The AI-agent builder cohort on X (Twitter): people shipping autonomous agents, posting about agent infrastructure, agent payments, and agent identity. Highest fit, because the algorithmic-manager idea is native to their feed.
- Hacker News: the Show HN and the comment threads on agent and DAO and Wyoming-LLC posts. The front-page post is held for the build phase per the v2 plan, but commenting genuinely and linking when relevant is fair now.
- AI-agent and autonomous-agent Discords and Slacks (the communities around the major agent frameworks). Direct, warm, and exactly the audience.
- Latent Space (newsletter and Discord), the AI Engineer community, and South Park Commons. Builder-dense, credibility-sensitive, right altitude.
- Indie Hackers and the solo-AI-founder corners of Reddit (r/AI_Agents and similar). Lower fit per head, higher volume.
- The Quorum of One existing audience, which is the warmest traffic we have and the first to point at the page.

## Sourcing method for the named list

When ready to send, gather thirty real, currently-active targets this way, not from memory:

1. Web-search current (2026) builders posting about AI agents that transact, agent identity, agent payments, and AI-run companies. Capture name, what they build, and a public contact or handle.
2. Pull the founders behind the agent-payment protocol moment (the people building on agent commerce rails), since legal-personhood is the missing layer next to what they already work on.
3. Scan the Quorum of One subscriber base for anyone who already fits, since that is warm.
4. Note anyone who has publicly asked whether an AI can legally run a company, because they have already named the pain.

Each entry gets a one-line reason it fits, so the send is personal and not a blast. Relational shape, never a funnel, per standing Ecodia outbound doctrine.
