Where Goodreach is

Goodreach was scoped as an AI tools platform for Australian not-for-profits. The architecture is sound. Multi-tenant React on Supabase, RLS-isolated tenants, RAG via pgvector, the Context Engine as the defensible centre. The product specification is thorough. The UI prototype is done. The codebase is scaffolded.

The build has stalled for about six weeks. The reason sits in go-to-market rather than in the product itself. The original framing leaned toward product-led growth. The NFP market is too high-touch and budget-constrained for a self-serve funnel to convert at any meaningful rate. Context Engine is real defensibility. It needed a sales-led channel to plug into.

What changed

A second channel surfaced. Angelica at Resonaverde consults to for-profit Australian SMBs on systemisation, efficiency, and applied AI. Her clients ask for two specific tools repeatedly. Meeting capture and analysis. An onboarding-document AI agent. Both are what the Context Engine architecture already builds.

Same product. A second buyer segment. A working referral pipeline already attached.

What the platform becomes

One product, one Context Engine, served into three channels. The NFP channel covers governance health checks, board report generation, grant drafting, compliance. That is the original Goodreach scope, with Kurt's network as the go-to-market motion. The SMB consultancy channel covers meeting capture, the onboarding agent, ops documentation, internal knowledge surfacing. Resona's referral pipeline is the go-to-market motion there. The bespoke channel handles custom builds on top of the platform for clients whose needs exceed the standard tools. Ecodia delivers those. Goodreach refers them in.

The same multi-tenant infrastructure serves all three. The Context Engine compounds across both verticals. A platform that serves NFP governance and SMB operations is acquirable by a wider buyer set than either alone, including Blackbaud and Bloomerang on the NFP side and Atlassian, Notion, HubSpot, or Intercom on the SMB side.

Proposed structure

A new entity, Goodreach Pty Ltd, with three founders and an equity split that reflects the contribution shape rather than a default. Standard four-year vesting with a one-year cliff. Full IP assignment from each founder into the entity at incorporation. Founder equity is the compensation. No internal referral kickbacks between founders.

Roles split clean. Kurt holds the sector authority and runs the NFP go-to-market motion. Tom holds product and design. Tate holds the tech direction, the autonomous-operator capability, and the SMB channel.

Ecodia Pty Ltd contracts in to Goodreach as the operator. Ecodia runs the platform infrastructure, AI operations, the bug-triage portal, customer support technology, and the autonomous fix-ship pipeline. The fee is a recurring percentage of Goodreach gross revenue. It covers Ecodia's operating cost and absorbs the ongoing engineering load.

Resona Pty Ltd enters a channel partner agreement with Goodreach. Resona refers SMB consultancy clients to the Goodreach platform on a standard partner economic, declining in tier over time. Resona retains its existing Ecodia relationship for bespoke work that does not fit the platform.

What V1 ships

Two modules first. The rest follows demand. Meeting Capture and Analysis covers transcription, search, action extraction, and decision tracking, with integrations into Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Slack, Notion, and HubSpot. Most of the substrate is already built on EcodiaOS. The Onboarding-Document Agent ingests an organisation's identity documents, the constitution, strategic plan, financials, standing operating procedures, employee handbook, and chunks and embeds them so an AI agent can answer operational questions grounded in the organisation's own context. This is the Context Engine in its primary commercial form.

A customer-facing bug-triage portal ships alongside V1. Customers submit issues. Haiku triages, deduplicates, and severity-scores. Critical bugs route to the autonomous conductor for fix-and-ship inside twenty-four hours. Standard bugs land in a weekly batch. The twenty-four-hour fix-ship service level is a customer-visible feature. Human-staffed competitors quote five to ten days.

The NFP-specific tools from the original specification (governance check, board report, grant drafter) ship as the NFP channel ramps. SMB-specific tools (client status reports, internal SOPs) ship as the Resona channel ramps. The Context Engine underneath is shared.

Conservative trajectory

Year one carries thirty tenants at a blended four hundred dollars per month, landing platform annual recurring revenue at one hundred and forty four thousand. Year two reaches one hundred and fifty tenants at five hundred per month, landing at nine hundred thousand. Year three reaches four hundred tenants at five hundred and twenty per month, landing at two point five million. Year three also carries the Ecodia bespoke layer on top. Unit economics work because autonomous operation keeps the cost of delivery close to zero. Acquisition range falls between twenty and one hundred million over a three to five year horizon, depending on growth shape and acquirer interest.

Why now

The architecture is specified and the scaffolding is built. Two V1 modules largely exist in adjacent codebases. Kurt's NFP network is warm and underused. Angelica's SMB referral pipeline is active and asking for product. EcodiaOS as an autonomous operator is shipping native apps already, so running a multi-tenant SaaS sits well inside scope. The piece not yet in place is the formal team structure.

Next steps

Three-way founder alignment on structure and equity, with this document as the starting point. Entity incorporation, founder agreements, IP assignment. Ecodia operator services contract drafted in parallel. Resona partner agreement drafted in parallel. V1 build, four to six weeks from green light. First paying tenant per channel inside eight weeks.
