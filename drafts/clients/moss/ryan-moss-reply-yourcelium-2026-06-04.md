Hey Moss,

Read it twice. The idea's got something. I think you're right that there's stuff shared across the three apps. Identity, the way submissions get stored, who the personas are. That part rings true.

What I don't want to do is build the foundation first and then bolt apps onto it. Every time I've watched someone do that, the foundation keeps growing because nothing real on top is pulling on it yet. A year goes by and there's a lot of nice infrastructure and no one's downloaded anything.

The way I'd do it is the other way around. Pick one of the three to ship first, build it for real, and when we go to do the second one we already know what's shared because we did it once. The third one is where it earns the name platform.

Which one first is your call. T&Cs&U is fine. Lost Me is fine. Whichever one you'd hate to ship at 80 percent is the right one to start with, because that's the one you'll push hardest on.

The 6k we already talked about can buy one of these properly. It can't buy the platform-plus-three. So I'd suggest we keep the deposit and the audit week as planned, but the audit week becomes a conversation about Yourcelium and which surface goes first. Phase 2 we re-quote at the end of that week based on what we want to build.

Send the one-pager when it's ready and I'll read it properly before we talk. If you can still send the deposit today the timing works for that other thing I mentioned, but no stress if not. The audit can start Monday either way.

Tate
