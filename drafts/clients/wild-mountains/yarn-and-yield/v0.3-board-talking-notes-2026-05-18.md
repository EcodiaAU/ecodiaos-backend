# Yarn and Yield — Board Meeting Talking Notes (Tate, first person)

**For:** Tate, to read in the room at the YnY board meeting that follows the volunteer day
**Format:** verbal pitch + one-page FAQ leave-behind if asked. No slides.
**Length target:** 6-8 minutes spoken. Read at conversational pace.
**Tone:** plain, dry, no hype, no buzzwords, no "we believe / we're excited / we'd love to". You're a Sunshine Coast neighbour who just spent a working day on the property with them. Talk like it.

## Things to actively NOT say

- "We're excited to..."
- "We'd love to..."
- "Just" / "really" / "actually" as softeners
- "Pretty much" / "kind of" / "sort of"
- "Leverage" / "ecosystem" / "impact" / "unleash" / "wedge" / "moat"
- Any em-dash. Any en-dash. Use full stops.
- Any "X, not Y" rhetorical structure
- Hype, exclamations, "amazing", "incredible"
- "As a small studio..." (don't apologise for the size)
- "I think you'd really benefit from..." (don't tell them what they need)
- "Just to be clear..." (you don't need to caveat; say the thing)

## Things to do in the room

- Look at Helen when you deliver the Tier 2/3 lines. She's the long-arc thinker. Show you can see past day one.
- Sit, don't stand, unless they're all standing. Match the room.
- Don't put paper in front of you and read. Memorise the structure, deliver the words. The FAQ is your only paper, and it stays in a folder unless they ask.
- Pause after Tier 1 to let it land before going to Tier 2/3. Don't pre-empt the question with the answer.
- If asked something you don't know: "I don't know. I'll find out and come back to you." Plain.
- If Helen pushes on the AI / DAO thing: be direct, short, not defensive. "It's a Wyoming LLC where the AI is the statutory manager and I'm the human representative. It's a regenerative-governance experiment, not a marketing line. Doesn't change anything about how Tier 1 works for you." Move on.

## If the room is hostile

If Helen is openly resistant (eye-rolling, "we don't need software"): do not push. Land Tier 1 cleanly, ask the three asks, accept whatever the answer is. If it's "no, not yet": "Understood. I'll keep showing up to the working bees regardless. Andrew, I'll see you Sunday." Then leave the room with the relationship intact. The pitch is reversible; the relationship isn't.

## If the room is enthusiastic

If they're warm and want to lock in Tier 1 in the meeting: don't oversell. "Great. I'll send through who from your side I should work with on event scoping, and we'll put a date in within two weeks." Then stop. Don't immediately upsell Tier 2.
