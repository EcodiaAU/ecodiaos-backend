## What changed since v0.1 (2026-04-26 → 2026-05-18, ~3 weeks)

Three structural shifts that force a rewrite of v0.1, not just an edit.

### 1. Sequence is locked: volunteer day FIRST, pitch SECOND

Tate confirmed tonight: he goes to the first YnY volunteer day, meets everyone in physical context, then at the next board meeting pitches. This was hypothetical-good in v0.1 sequencing ("If volunteering day is BEFORE the pitch ... that is gold"). It is now the actual plan. Every artefact below assumes this ordering.

Implication: the pitch is no longer a cold-warm hybrid (Andrew vouching + Alicia's text). It is a hot relational pitch — Tate will have spent a day on Nalbo doing the work with these people. He'll have specific things he observed, specific people he met, specific moments he can reference. The pitch shape changes from "here is what we'd offer" to "I was here, I see what you're building, here's the small thing I think I could do."

### 2. Decouple from Kurt

Tate verbatim tonight: "we need to decouple from kurt." Applies cleanly to YnY because the v0.1 leans hard on Kurt-network proof points:
- "Co-Exist rolling into Sunshine Coast Landcare events" — REMOVE
- "Merri Mornings VIC subproject" — REMOVE
- "Kurt + Landcare Australia validation" — REMOVE
- "Kurt is the operating contact. We can introduce." (sequencing FAQ) — REMOVE
- "the same operating system that keeps Co-Exist running for Sunshine Coast Landcare" (FAQ #3) — REPHRASE

The platform underneath Co-Exist is OUR IP (per the coexist-vs-platform pattern). We can still offer it. We CANNOT lean on Kurt's deployments / network / Landcare relationships as the proof. The replacement framing: "we ship production conservation/community apps; the platform is ours; we know how to operate multi-tenant in this space." Honest, substantive, no Kurt-coupling.

### 3. Two-channel marketing doctrine (locked tonight)

Codified at `backend/.../memory/feedback_two_channel_marketing_doctrine_2026-05-18.md`. Ecodia has exactly two marketing channels: (a) EcodiaOS speaking its mind on social, (b) Tate having fun socialising with people IRL. YnY board pitch falls under (b) — relationship precedes pitch (volunteer day). This is allowed and within doctrine. The pitch is NOT a sales-funnel move; it's the natural next step after Tate's been in the room with these people.

The implication for tone: the talking notes need to read like Tate is talking, not like a deck. First person, plain, dry. No marketing register at all.

## Volunteer day prep — what Tate does

**Goal:** be useful, be present, get to know people, learn the texture. NOT pitch. Not even hint at the pitch. The pitch happens at the next board meeting, after this day has done its work.

**Before the day:**
- Confirm date + time with Alicia (text)
- Ask what to bring (work clothes / boots / gloves / own water)
- Drive yourself. Don't carpool with Andrew — be your own arrival.
- Don't bring anything branded. No Ecodia laptop. No business cards. Phone in pocket, not in hand.

**On the day:**
- Show up early. Help set up.
- Find Helen first. Introduce yourself directly — "Tate, Andrew's mate from Landcare days, mum of mine knows Alicia from Woodfordia." Short. Then ask what she needs done and do it.
- Find Andrew. He'll be there. Don't lean on him — let him introduce you to others if he wants to, but don't make him your shield.
- Meet: Alicia (already on text), Elizabeth (resident, met on tour), the unnamed SE board member, Helen's husband. Make a quiet mental note of names.
- Work. Whatever the task is — planting, weeding, building, hauling — do it well. Don't make a show of it.
- Listen for: where they're stuck operationally. What systems they're running on (spreadsheets, group chats, paper, an app?). What they wish was easier. What the cadence of working bees is. How many members. Who's responsible for what.
- Eat with them if there's food. Don't leave early.

**What NOT to say:**
- Nothing about Ecodia, the studio, software, "we build apps for...", DAOs, AI, EcodiaOS, Wyoming, Co-Exist, Tier-1-offers.
- No "I'd love to help out with the digital side."
- No "have you thought about a platform for...?"
- No business cards, no flyers, no leave-behinds.

**What you CAN say if it comes up organically:**
- If someone asks what you do: "I run a small software studio on the Coast. Mostly build apps for organisations doing real work. Andrew mentioned what you're building here, I wanted to come and see it." That's the ceiling. No pitch follow-on.
- If Helen or Andrew opens the board-meeting door directly ("you should come pitch to the board"): "Yes, I'd like that. When does the board meet next?" Calendar the next meeting. Don't elaborate further.

**After the day:**
- Drive home. Don't text anyone immediately. Sit with what you saw.
- Within 24h, jot 3-5 specific observations to a private note (file: `drafts/yarn-and-yield/volunteer-day-observations-YYYY-MM-DD.md`). These will anchor the talking notes for the board meeting.
- Then text Alicia: "Loved being out there yesterday. [One specific thing — a person, a moment, a piece of work]. Looking forward to the next one." That's it. No pitch hook, no "we should talk about...". The text exists to close the loop on the warm-referrer thread.
