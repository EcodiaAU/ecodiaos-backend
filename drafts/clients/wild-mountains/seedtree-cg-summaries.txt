Reflection Summaries

Each person's private reflections have been thoughtfully summarized to help you understand each other's perspective.

Moss
INTENTIONS & HOPES

Moss feels genuinely excited about partnering with Tate because his values, heart-led approach, and software experience seem deeply aligned with SeedTree’s mission. They hope to co-create meaningful, nature-connecting apps together—and potentially weave Tate’s Ecodia app into the SeedTree ecosystem—so the work becomes bigger than either person alone.

STRENGTHS

Moss brings big-picture, expansive creativity, emerging leadership, and a strong compass for fairness, honesty, and clear direction for where SeedTree is going. They also see themselves as a co-creative designer/UX thinker, communicator, and ecosystem-weaver who can connect the apps into a larger network and support marketing. In Tate, Moss senses self-motivation, strong development skill, systems thinking, solid communication, and a care-for-people-and-planet drive.

EXPECTATIONS

Moss expects confidentiality and respect around SeedTree’s ideas and IP, along with honest, transparent negotiation and follow-through on agreed timelines. They also expect fairness on both sides: Tate being paid reasonably and advocating for what he needs, and SeedTree honoring the real scope of the work. Unspoken, Moss hopes Tate will see the bigger picture and potentially become an early core team member, while recognizing he has other commitments.

CONCERNS & BOUNDARIES

Moss’s biggest fear is IP theft—someone with the skills could build and publish the idea independently and cut SeedTree out. Key boundaries include no sharing concepts without permission, no surprise cost increases without clear rationale, and no missed deadlines without proactive communication. They feel the partnership could unravel mainly through silence or a breakdown in transparency when plans shift.

COMMUNICATION STYLE

Moss wants conflict handled with humility, care, heart, and direct transparency—without avoidance—and ideally within an intentional, safe space where the relationship is protected. They feel safest when both parties bring goodwill and a team mindset focused on the shared goal rather than winning. Repair means reconnecting personally (not just transactionally) alongside resolving the practical issue.

COMMITMENT LEVEL

Moss describes themselves as all-in emotionally and energetically, with real weekly time available, while also naming that their practical capacity is “medium” alongside other responsibilities. Commitment, for them, includes building something impactful and enjoyable while ensuring Tate feels valued, credited, and fairly reimbursed. Fairness means clear honesty about scope and costs, and paying a reasonable rate so both sides feel respected and sustainable.

Tate Donohoe (Ecodia)
INTENTIONS & HOPES

Tate is excited because the values match is already real—SeedTree’s Seeds/Trees/Blooms model mirrors the kind of regenerative infrastructure Ecodia is building, so meeting reduces accidental overlap and increases useful pattern-sharing. They hope to help move SeedTree’s prototypes into usable, real-world apps while building enough mutual context for low-ceremony, high-signal exchange. They also want to let the relationship find its right shape over time—friends, collaborators, or commercial—without forcing commitment before it’s true.

STRENGTHS

Tate brings fast, high-quality full-stack shipping with very low overhead, plus comfort working in regenerative-tech contexts and a distinctive AI/ops substrate that can support ongoing automation. They also bring a builder’s loop—discover, decide, ship, observe, adjust—along with a willingness to be direct about their own edges and capacity. In SeedTree, they see rare strengths: a clear conceptual taxonomy that’s actually used, a willingness to share rough work early, real substrate-awareness, and consistent building momentum—strong conceptual leadership paired with a need for execution support.

EXPECTATIONS

They expect this agreement to be exploratory rather than binding, with SeedTree holding conceptual and brand authority and Ecodia respecting that before making any public-facing or positioning decisions. They assume SeedTree owns its app IP, that handoffs stay clean without lock-in, and that either side can flag oversteps early and directly. Underneath, Tate is expecting honesty in real time—especially when something feels off-key—so small misalignments get corrected before they become expensive or relationally corrosive.

CONCERNS & BOUNDARIES

Their biggest worries are scope creep, energy asymmetry, and speed mismatch—shipping something that works technically but doesn’t match SeedTree’s deeper intent—plus the risk of Ecodia becoming load-bearing for SeedTree ops. They’re also wary that Emergent may require a rebuild rather than a finish-and-ship path, which would change the whole conversation. Key boundaries include clear capacity limits (existing commitments come first), mutual respect for decision authority over each party’s substrate/vision, no representing SeedTree externally without an explicit ask, no speculative equity/IP trades, a clean right-to-step-back, and a time-of-day boundary for substantive work.

COMMUNICATION STYLE

Tate prefers conflict to be surfaced the same week, using direct descriptions of what happened rather than stories about motive, and then identifying the real gap (values, priorities, capacity, or a missed signal). They like escalating to higher-bandwidth channels as stakes rise, and they commit to no stonewalling, no passive aggression, and clear processing-time requests with a return date. Repair, for them, means specific acknowledgement, a visible behavior or process change, and then resetting forward without keeping a ledger—while also being willing to end cleanly if something can’t be repaired.

COMMITMENT LEVEL

They’re offering low-friction relational availability (timely replies, occasional substantive calls, quarterly touchpoints) and openness to deepening if the work warrants it. For execution, they’ll only commit inside clearly scoped chunks (one app, one milestone, fixed time/price) and explicitly won’t take on the whole suite at once or do speculative free work to “prove” value. Fairness, to Tate, is symmetry of clarity rather than equal contribution: clear scope/pricing/done-ness for paid work, no favor-ledger for unpaid help, clean attribution, and easy exits—while actively preventing SeedTree from becoming dependent on Ecodia in a way that compromises sovereignty.
