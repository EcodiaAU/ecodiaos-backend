## 3. Background to the demand

### 3.1 Cost-reducing offers made by Ecodia and declined by you

Prior to and during the engagement, Ecodia made multiple offers intended to reduce the total cost payable by Spatial & Compliance Pty Ltd. Each was declined.

1. **Fixed-price quote for the Phase 2 ticket.** Ecodia's initial quote for the engagement was $80.00 per hour. After you scoped the Phase 2 Cognito integration as a discrete ticket and sent it through by email, Ecodia replied by email with a fixed-price quote of **approximately $1,600.00 inc GST** for that ticket. In a subsequent phone call you declined the fixed-price option and elected to proceed on hourly terms at $80.00 per hour. The hourly engagement subsequently produced INV-2026-002 at $3,432.00 inc GST. Had the fixed-price offer been accepted, the total payable to Ecodia for the same scope of work would have been approximately half the amount now in dispute.

2. **Workflow proposal to reduce billable hours.** During the engagement Ecodia proposed an alternative workflow under which Ecodia would deliver larger units of scope per pull request rather than ticket-by-ticket. Ecodia advised that the proposed workflow would reduce billable hours by reducing per-ticket context-load overhead, and would reduce the frequency of revision cycles by your reviewer. You declined the proposal and elected to continue with the ticket-by-ticket workflow.

The cost outcome of which you now complain is the direct consequence of your election to proceed on hourly terms and on a ticket-by-ticket basis after being offered both a fixed-price alternative and a lower-hours workflow alternative.

### 3.2 Payment chronology

On **28 April 2026** Ecodia sent a payment reminder by email to craige@fireauditors.com.au, with the invoice re-attached.

On **3 May 2026** you replied disputing the hours claimed and requesting a breakdown of time personally spent on the work.

On **5 May 2026** Ecodia provided an itemised breakdown of the 39 hours by date and task. That email also offered two paths to resolve the matter:

1. Payment of INV-2026-002 in full within 14 days, with a clean handover comment placed on PR 212; or
2. Particularised dispute identifying the specific deliverable said not to have been provided.

You did not take either path. Your reply of 5 May 2026 declined to pay the invoice, did not identify any specific deliverable said to be deficient, and concluded the engagement.

### 3.3 Identity of the contracting party and selection of tools

Your correspondence of 3 May 2026 and 5 May 2026 asserts that the engagement was a personal hire of Tate Donohoe as an individual contractor, and that the use of artificial intelligence tooling by Ecodia was a breach of the engagement. Both assertions are denied.

1. **Contracting party.** The advertisement on Facebook to which you responded was published by **Ecodia Pty Ltd** and offered software development services performed by Ecodia. The invoices issued to you, including INV-2026-002, were issued in the name of Ecodia Pty Ltd and bore the ABN of Ecodia Pty Ltd. Payment was requested into a bank account in the name of Ecodia Pty Ltd. The engagement at all times was between Spatial & Compliance Pty Ltd and Ecodia Pty Ltd, and not between Spatial & Compliance Pty Ltd and Tate Donohoe in his personal capacity.

2. **Selection of tools.** In the absence of an express written term to the contrary, the selection of tools, methods, software, personnel and processes used by Ecodia to perform contracted software development work is at the sole discretion of Ecodia. No term was agreed restricting Ecodia's use of any particular development tool, including but not limited to artificial intelligence assistants. The hourly rate of $80.00 inc GST was charged for Ecodia's time on the engagement, billed at a substantial discount to Ecodia's standard rate, and was not a rate for the personal manual labour of any specific individual.

The development methodology used by Ecodia, and the involvement of artificial intelligence tooling, are not a defence to payment for work delivered into your codebase and accepted. The work product of INV-2026-002 remains in your repository at `bitbucket.org/fireauditors1/be` and your team has had the use and benefit of it since the date of delivery.

No specific contractual or factual basis for non-payment has been provided at any time.
