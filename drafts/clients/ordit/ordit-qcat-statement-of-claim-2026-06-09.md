## E. Acceptance by conduct and ratification

13. The Respondent's technical lead approved the deliverable on 24 April 2026 (paragraph 10 above). The Respondent's continued use of the merged code in production from 24 April 2026 to the date of this application is acceptance by conduct of the work performed.

14. After Eugene Kerner's objection on 23 April 2026 raised the use of artificial intelligence development tooling, the Applicant's director Tate Donohoe spoke with the Respondent's director Craige Hills in person on multiple occasions. 15. A party who, with full knowledge of the facts said to give rise to an objection, accepts the benefit of a transaction and authorises its continuance is bound by it. The Respondent's director, the contracting principal and the billing contact named on the invoice, authorised continued performance after the issue now raised in defence had been put to him. The Respondent cannot now refuse to pay for work it authorised after disclosure.

## I. Anticipated defences

29. The Applicant anticipates the following defences and addresses them briefly. Each is more fully addressed in the Applicant's evidence at the conciliation conference and hearing.

(a) **Selection of tools.** In the absence of an express written term to the contrary, the selection of tools, methods, software, personnel and processes used to perform contracted software development work is at the sole discretion of the developer. No term was agreed restricting the Applicant's use of any particular development tool. The advertisement which the Respondent's director saw and to which he responded by direct message listed "AI and admin automation" in the offering. The Applicant's director verbally disclosed the use of artificial intelligence tooling to the Respondent's director after the objection of 23 April 2026, and the Respondent's director expressly authorised continued work after that disclosure (paragraph 14 above).

(b) **The work is substandard.** The Respondent's own technical lead approved pull request 212 on 24 April 2026. The deliverable has been in the Respondent's production environment for more than six weeks at the date of filing without any deficient deliverable being particularised. Out of scope changes raised in the 23 April 2026 objection were reverted within 14 minutes.

(c) **Eugene Kerner's initial objection means the work was not accepted.** Eugene Kerner approved the same code one day after the objection, after the in scope revert. The Respondent's director, the billing contact named on the invoice, authorised continued work in person. A superseded technical lead objection cannot void a director's acceptance.

(d) **No signed scope of work exists.** Verbal contracts are enforceable in this Tribunal. The $80.00 per hour rate was communicated and accepted in writing by email and was never disputed during the engagement. Continued performance at the agreed rate by both parties constitutes acceptance of terms.

(e) **The hours billed are inflated.** On 5 May 2026 the Applicant provided an itemised 39 hour breakdown by date and task. The Respondent did not particularise any time entry as inaccurate when invited to do so. Failure to particularise is a waiver of hours specific objections.

(f) **GST was unlawfully charged.** The ABR record confirms the Applicant has been GST registered continuously since 03 February 2026, more than two months before the invoice date.

(g) **The engagement was personal not corporate.** The advertisement was published by Ecodia Pty Ltd. The invoice was issued by Ecodia Pty Ltd. The bank account nominated for payment was in the name of Ecodia Pty Ltd. Payment was demanded by Ecodia Pty Ltd. There is no plausible reading on which the Respondent thought it had engaged Tate Donohoe in a personal capacity.
