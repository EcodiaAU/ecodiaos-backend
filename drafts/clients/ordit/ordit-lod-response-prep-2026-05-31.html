the spine

The case rests on ratification by conduct. After Eugene Kerner blocked the pull request on 23 April 2026 with the comment that an AI agent had introduced unreviewed work, Tate spoke with Craige Hills in person on multiple occasions. Tate disclosed the use of AI development tools. Craige verbally accepted and authorised continued work. Work continued. The legal doctrine is straightforward. A party that, with full knowledge of the facts, accepts the benefit of a transaction is bound by it. Post-objection ratification is the strongest variant, because the counterparty cannot later claim ignorance of the issue they raise as a defence. Every plausible defence Craige could mount collapses against this fact.

anticipated defences

I did not consent to AI work.
The Facebook post Craige responded to lists AI and admin automation as a service category. AI capability was disclosed at inducement. Multiple in-person disclosures after Eugene's 23 April objection. Continued authorisation of work and continued benefit from the merged code constitute ratification by conduct.
The work is substandard because it was AI generated.
Eugene Kerner, the client's own technical lead, approved PR 212 on 24 April 2026 at 10:39 AEST. The approval is public, timestamped, and attributed on Bitbucket. Out-of-scope changes were reverted within hours of Eugene's objection (commit 24ab453). The code has been operating in production for over five weeks. Eugene's stated standard was line-by-line human review before submission. Tate met that standard.
Eugene's rejection means the work was not accepted.
Eugene rejected on 23 April. Eugene approved the same code on 24 April, after the in-scope revert. Craige is the bill payer. Eugene is the technical reviewer, with no contracting authority. The bill payer subsequently authorised continued work in person. A superseded technical-lead objection cannot void a bill-payer's acceptance.
No signed scope of work exists.
Verbal contracts are enforceable in QCAT. The $80 per hour rate was communicated and never disputed. Continued performance at the agreed rate constitutes acceptance of terms. Eugene's approval of PR 212 establishes acceptance of the deliverable.
The hours billed are inflated.
On 5 May, Ecodia provided an itemised 39-hour breakdown by date and task and offered two paths: pay within fourteen days or particularise the dispute. Craige did not particularise. Failure to particularise when invited to do so is a waiver of hours-specific objections.
The Facebook post implied solo human development (Australian Consumer Law s18 misleading conduct).
The post lists AI and admin automation in the offering. First-person framing in a small-business pitch is standard positioning and not a contractual representation of solo execution. The inducement document itself disclosed AI capability. Subsequent verbal disclosures and continued engagement cured any residual ambiguity.
GST was unlawfully charged on a non-registered ABN.
Ecodia Pty Ltd backdated its GST registration. The ATO permits backdating up to four years. The invoice is GST-compliant. The ABR record will confirm registration covers 19 April 2026.
The LoD is defective, wrong entity, or improperly signed.
The creditor (Ecodia Pty Ltd) is correctly named. The debtor (Spatial & Compliance Pty Ltd) is correctly named. The sole Director of Ecodia Pty Ltd signed. The EcodiaOS co-signature was authorised by the Director and reflects the Wyoming DAO LLC's algorithmic-manager status under W.S. 17-31-104. QCAT minor civil disputes jurisdiction is correct: debt under $25,000, both parties in Queensland.
Counter-claim for damages caused by the work.
No deliverable was named as deficient when the breakdown was offered on 5 May. The code has been in production use without reported defect for over five weeks. Any counter-claim would need particularised damages that were never raised at the time. Stop and consult a Queensland-admitted solicitor before responding to a counter-claim of any kind.
