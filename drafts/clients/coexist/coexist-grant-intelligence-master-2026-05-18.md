## Strategic insights that should change Kurt's behaviour

These are the load-bearing intel pieces. Internalise these before any application work.

### 1. Co-Exist's signature differentiator is "youth-led" not "youth-serving"

The only Australian funder that *mandates* applicant orgs be youth-led is **Foundation for Young Australians (FYA)**. Their Youth Action & Wellbeing Grants require leadership by people under 30 with lived experience. Co-Exist's 7-role pipeline (participant → assist-leader → co-leader → leader → national_staff → national_admin → super_admin) IS that proof verbatim.

But the value of this framing extends beyond FYA. Across every funder reviewed, the vast majority of "youth" applicants are youth-*serving* orgs run by 40-60 year-old social-sector professionals. Co-Exist is one of a small handful of genuinely youth-led national orgs. That distinction differentiates every application.

**Action:** lead every application narrative with "Co-Exist is one of Australia's only genuinely youth-led national conservation organisations - the leadership pipeline is the proof."

### 2. There is no dedicated "MRV / impact-infrastructure" grant line in Australia 2026

No funder runs a grant category explicitly for impact-measurement infrastructure for conservation NFPs. The closest verbatim language is **NSW Environmental Trust R&R** which funds "Enhanced Environmental Management - improving data, connectivity, and resource management." That program is the strongest single home for the impact-tracking pitch.

For all other applications, the platform/portal build must ride as the *enabling layer* inside a broader restoration / community / capacity grant - not pitched standalone as MRV infrastructure.

**Action:** the platform never leads the pitch. The leadership pipeline, on-ground conservation outcomes, and youth empowerment lead. The platform is what *enables* those outcomes to scale.

### 3. The closed-foundation trap

Post-2020, almost every major Australian family foundation moved to "trust-based philanthropy" or "proactive granting" - they pick grantees, grantees don't pick them. Confirmed closed / invitation-only across this audit: **Paul Ramsay Foundation**, **Sidney Myer Fund / Myer Foundation Sustainability** (no unsolicited), **Reichstein Foundation**, **William Buckland Foundation**, **R.E. Ross Trust**, **Yulgilbar Foundation Fund** (final round done), **Hugh Williamson Foundation**, **Tim Fairfax Family Foundation**, **English Family Foundation** (all programs), **Pratt Foundation**, **VFFF for orgs** (individuals only via Backing the Future).

**Cold applications to closed foundations = wasted cycles.** The way IN is warm intro via existing grantees, philanthropic advisers, or shared board members.

**Action:** ask Lisa Miller / Cam Adams at Wedgetail who they know on Ian Potter, Sidney Myer, and VFFF boards. Don't fill out forms for foundations that don't accept them.

### 4. Wedgetail is the most aligned warm relationship Co-Exist already has

Wedgetail VC's thesis is biodiversity outcomes via tech-platform + on-ground action. Their portfolio is 40% tech (Xylo Systems, Agronomeye, Sumday - natural capital measurement) and 60% land-based. The impact tracking system maps directly onto the tech-platform portfolio segment.

The March 2026 deck is the warm lead. Re-engage with a **grant-shaped ask first** ($35-50k catalytic capacity grant), positioning the platform as measurement substrate that makes future equity-stage scale possible. Wedgetail uses grants for early-stage de-risking before equity.

**Action:** Kurt re-engages Lisa/Cam with the grant ask in Q4 2026 once Tier A is delivered (clean substrate proves the team can execute).

### 5. The infrastructure stack pays for itself before the first grant

Total estimated value: AU$25-50k/year of infrastructure that Co-Exist would otherwise pay for or struggle without. Free. Register before any grant application is submitted.
