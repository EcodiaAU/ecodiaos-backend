# Co-Exist Australia - Tech Empowerment Grants Intelligence Brief

**Compiled:** 2026-05-18
**Lane:** NFP digital transformation / tech-for-good grants, 2026-2027 cycle
**Client:** Co-Exist Australia Ltd (ABN 39 660 776 983), ACNC-registered, QLD
**Funding target:** AU$35,000-$50,000 to build unified impact tracking + leadership/attendee portals

---

## Executive summary

Co-Exist's profile (ACNC-registered, youth-led, national footprint, conservation outcomes, existing React/Capacitor/Supabase platform, measurable impact baseline) is unusually well-suited to tech-for-good funders right now. The five candidates to prioritise:

1. **AWS IMAGINE Grant ANZ 2026** - the single best fit. ACNC eligible, cloud-leveraging projects, up to USD $75K cash + USD $25K credits + AWS technical engagement. Round One application window opened April 2026, closes 5 June 2026 - meaning Co-Exist should target the **2027 cycle (opens March 2027)** unless we can submit in the next two weeks.
2. **FRRR Larger Leverage / Strengthening Rural Communities** - up to AU$50,000 for digital skill-building + connectivity projects. Round 30 opens 25 June 2026, closes 17 September 2026. Co-Exist's regional collective footprint is a clean alignment.
3. **Telstra Connected Communities (via FRRR)** - AU$10K grants, AU$200K pool. Light application, broad eligibility (NFP/charity), digital-literacy + community-connection framing fits Co-Exist's portal build. 2026 round closed 26 March; **flag for 2027 round (opens late Feb 2027)**.
4. **Atlassian Solution Grants** - up to 25 free expert consultancy days on Atlassian implementation (Jira/Confluence). Not cash, but offsets ~AU$30-60K of work-management build cost if Co-Exist's leadership portal could ride on Jira/Confluence rather than custom. Rolling application, no deadline.
5. **Microsoft + Google + AWS donated infrastructure stack via Connecting Up (Infoxchange/TechSoup)** - not a cash grant but the foundational tech-credit stack: Microsoft 365 + Azure credits, Google Workspace + Ad Grants (USD$10K/mo search ad budget), AWS Nonprofit credits. Co-Exist should have all three registered before the quote document is delivered - it makes the cash quote look smaller by carving out infrastructure from billable scope.

The Atlassian Foundation's direct grant-making is education-focused and not openly Australian-NFP-applicable in 2026 (no open public application cycle). Vodafone Australia's foundation has been renamed TPG Telecom Foundation and is not running an obvious open tech-grant round. Optus "Donate Your Data" is consumer-side, not an NFP cash grant. Verified-discontinued/not-running section at the end.

## Active 2026-2027 programs

### 1. AWS IMAGINE Grant Australia/New Zealand 2026

- **Funder:** Amazon Web Services
- **Funding range (AUD equiv):** Up to ~AU$115K cash (USD $75K) + ~AU$38K AWS credits (USD $25K) + AWS specialist engagement (kickoff workshops, ongoing technical support)
- **Cycle / 2026 deadline:** Round One application portal opened April 2026, closes **5 June 2026**. Annual single cycle. Next cycle: March 2027.
- **Eligibility:** Registered NFP with active ACNC charity status (Co-Exist qualifies). Any annual budget size eligible. Educational institutions (incl. K-12, uni) excluded - Co-Exist is fine.
- **What it funds:** Cash + AWS promotional credits + technical support. Cash is unrestricted to project implementation.
- **Application complexity:** **Medium-heavy.** Two-round: Round One ~5-page proposal, Round Two (by invitation) full project plan.
- **Co-Exist alignment angle:** Unified impact tracking is a textbook IMAGINE pitch - measurable conservation outcomes (36K+ trees, 11K+ hours), AI/analytics ambitions (impact prediction, volunteer routing), scalable across collectives nationally. Supabase already runs on AWS infrastructure. Story: "from spreadsheets to a national youth-led conservation data platform."
- **URL:** https://aws.amazon.com/government-education/nonprofits/aws-imagine-grant-program/aws-imagine-grant-anz/
- **Verification:** Confirmed open 2026 cycle via AWS official page. Application portal live via apply.younoodle.com/showcase/competition/2026_aws_imagine_grant.

### 2. FRRR Strengthening Rural Communities (SRC) - Small & Vital + Larger Leverage

- **Funder:** Foundation for Rural & Regional Renewal (FRRR)
- **Funding range:** Small & Vital up to AU$10K; Larger Leverage up to AU$50K (digital skills/connectivity stream specifically funds NFP digital transformation)
- **Cycle / 2026 deadlines:** Current round closes 5pm AEDT 24 June 2026. Round 30 opens 25 June 2026, closes 17 September 2026, announced end Nov 2026.
- **Eligibility:** NFPs and community groups; preference for projects in regional/rural Australia. Co-Exist's regional collectives qualify; the metro-only framing must be avoided.
- **What it funds:** Cash grants. Larger Leverage stream explicitly funds digital tools, training infrastructure, connectivity.
- **Application complexity:** **Light-medium.** SmartyGrants-style portal, ~3-4 page proposal.
- **Co-Exist alignment angle:** Frame as building digital infrastructure for regional youth conservation collectives - portal + impact tracking lets remote collectives self-organise, report up, plan tree-plantings/clean-ups without metro-centred admin overhead.
- **URL:** https://frrr.org.au/funding/src-small-vital/ and https://frrr.org.au/funding/
- **Verification:** Confirmed via FRRR site, current funding round listed for 2026.

### 3. Telstra Connected Communities Grants (administered by FRRR)

- **Funder:** Telstra Foundation (via FRRR)
- **Funding range:** Up to AU$10,000 per grant; total pool AU$200,000 (2026 round)
- **Cycle / 2026 deadlines:** 2026 round opened 24 Feb 2026, closed 26 March 2026, winners announced June 2026. **2027 round expected late Feb 2027 - prime target.**
- **Eligibility:** NFP community organisations, charities, local councils, education/health entities. Focus on digital literacy, social connection, cyber security, sustainability, disaster preparedness.
- **What it funds:** Cash. 12-month project window.
- **Application complexity:** **Light.** Single-round SmartyGrants form.
- **Co-Exist alignment angle:** Leadership portal trains young collective leaders in digital tools - aligns with "digital literacy + sustainability" twin focus areas. Frame around upskilling 18-30 collective leaders in tech-enabled organising.
- **URL:** https://frrr.org.au/funding/telstra-connected-communities-program/
- **Verification:** Confirmed 2026 cycle via FRRR/Telstra pages.

### 4. Atlassian Solution Grants (in-kind consultancy)

- **Funder:** Atlassian Foundation
- **Funding range:** Up to 25 free consultancy days with an Atlassian expert (estimated value AU$30-60K depending on day-rate equiv).
- **Cycle:** Rolling intake, limited capacity.
- **Eligibility:** Nonprofits/social enterprises with 25+ team members, already using or committed to using Atlassian apps (Jira/Confluence/etc).
- **What it funds:** Expert consultancy days only. No cash. Plus 50-100% off Atlassian app licensing.
- **Application complexity:** **Medium.** Rolling form, expects existing Atlassian footprint or clear adoption plan.
- **Co-Exist alignment angle:** If the leadership portal is built on Confluence (knowledge base) + Jira (events/tasks for collectives) instead of fully custom, this grant funds the implementation labour. Reduces the AU$35-50K cash ask by carving out the work-management layer. Worth proposing as a parallel infrastructure stream rather than a replacement for cash funding.
- **URL:** https://www.atlassian.com/teams/nonprofits/support-programs/solution-form
- **Verification:** Confirmed active via Atlassian for Nonprofits page.

### 5. Microsoft for Nonprofits (Tech for Social Impact)

- **Funder:** Microsoft
- **Funding range:** Donated Microsoft 365 Business Premium licenses, Azure credits (~USD $2K-$3.5K/year), Dynamics 365 discounts, Copilot offers (rolling out 2026).
- **Cycle:** Rolling. Apply once, eligibility re-verified annually.
- **Eligibility:** ACNC-registered charities; standard public-benefit mission test.
- **What it funds:** Product donations + credits. No cash.
- **Application complexity:** **Light.** Online portal via Microsoft Nonprofit Hub; verification via TechSoup/Connecting Up.
- **Co-Exist alignment angle:** Free Microsoft 365 for staff + volunteer leadership. Azure credits could host non-Supabase tooling. This is infrastructure subsidy - quote it as deducted from total project cost.
- **URL:** https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/nonprofits and verification via https://www.connectingup.org/
- **Verification:** Confirmed active 2026 via Microsoft Nonprofits page.

### 6. Google for Nonprofits + Google Ad Grants

- **Funder:** Google.org / Google for Nonprofits
- **Funding range:** Google Workspace free; Google Ad Grants up to USD $10,000/month (~AU$15K/mo) in Search ad spend; YouTube Nonprofit Program.
- **Cycle:** Rolling.
- **Eligibility:** ACNC-registered OR DGR-status OR income-tax-exempt NFP. Co-Exist qualifies. Excludes schools, government, hospitals.
- **What it funds:** In-kind ads + product donations. No cash.
- **Application complexity:** **Light** initially (Workspace, ~3-4 weeks verification). Ad Grants requires ongoing compliance (5% CTR, conversion tracking, monthly account activity).
- **Co-Exist alignment angle:** AU$15K/month in Search ads to drive volunteer signups and donations is a sustained channel; standing offer worth registering even if not part of the AU$35-50K platform build quote.
- **URL:** https://www.google.com/nonprofits/
- **Verification:** Confirmed active 2026 via multiple Australian agency guides (Marzipan Media 2026 edition, etc).

### 7. Google.org Impact Challenge: AI for Science 2026

- **Funder:** Google.org
- **Funding range:** USD $500K - $3M (~AU$770K - AU$4.6M)
- **Cycle / 2026 deadline:** Applications closed **1 May 2026**. Likely 2027 cycle similar window (Feb-May 2027).
- **Eligibility:** Global, including Australia. Nonprofits, social enterprises, academic institutions using AI for health or climate science breakthroughs.
- **What it funds:** Cash grant + Google.org Accelerator participation + technical mentorship + cloud resources.
- **Application complexity:** **Heavy.** Multi-stage, requires AI-research-grade technical proposal.
- **Co-Exist alignment angle:** Stretch fit - Co-Exist would need to position the impact-tracking platform as an AI-enabled biodiversity/restoration data system (e.g. ML on tree-survival prediction, volunteer-impact correlation). Only worth chasing 2027 cycle with a strong technical co-applicant (university partner, e.g. via Wedgetail VC network).

- **Verification:** Confirmed 2026 cycle ran via Google.org official page. 2027 cycle TBC.

## Recommended sequencing for Co-Exist

1. **Immediately:** Register Connecting Up + Microsoft + Google + AWS NFP programs (1-2 weeks total elapsed). Bakes infrastructure subsidy into the quote.
2. **June 2026:** Submit FRRR SRC (close 24 June) and/or Round 30 prep (opens 25 June).
3. **August-September 2026:** Submit NAB Foundation Community Grants Round 2 (Aug 17 - Sept 14) - the AU$50K Impact Grant tier matches the upper Co-Exist ask exactly.
4. **October-December 2026:** Submit Perpetual IMPACT application + LMCF Q4 round.
5. **Q1 2027 prep:** Atlassian Solution Grants (rolling), then **AWS IMAGINE 2027** as the flagship play (Round One opens March 2027). This is the highest-leverage single grant and warrants 6-8 weeks of proposal preparation.

The AU$35-50K target is achievable through 2-3 of these in parallel (e.g. FRRR Larger Leverage AU$50K alone covers it; NAB Foundation Impact Grant AU$50K alone covers it). The AWS IMAGINE 2027 play is the standalone upside swing that could fund the full vision plus a year-two expansion.
