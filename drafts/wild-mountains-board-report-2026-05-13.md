### Knowledge Transfer

Meg holds site operations, supplier relationships, compliance, community history, and Blue Card requirements for interns. Much of it is not documented. Trello checklists exist but are not detailed enough for a new CM to operate independently.

**Architecture (Tate leads technical build, working directly with Meg):**
- Layer 1: Word documents across 8 domains: site ops, program delivery, staffing, accommodation, bookings, finance/admin, compliance, community

Meg is open and engaged. Trello used during extraction sessions only. Word docs are the durable home. Starts this week.

## Stream 2: Leadership Residency

### Decisions Made (Day 2)

| Decision | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Schools phased out | Too logistically demanding, too thin on margin, dependent on Meg's specialist background. |
| One hero product first | Wild Mountains Leadership Residency. Build it profitably and exceptionally before expanding. |
| No WELA partnership at this stage | Wild Mountains builds its own program first. |
| Full ticket: $4,200 | External funders subsidise scholarship places. WM receives same revenue regardless of who pays. |
| Sequential cohorts only | 3-4 week gaps between cohorts for staff rest, admin, site reset. No overlapping cohorts on-site. |
| Handover model | Each cohort creates a handover pack (project log, site guide, letter to next cohort). No person-to-person overlap required. |
| Participant intake | TAFE-style: EOI, shortlist, 20-min interview, offer, enrolment, pre-arrival onboarding (2 weeks before arrival). |

**Funder model:** External funders pay $4,200 to subsidise a participant's access. WM receives $25,200 per cohort regardless of the funder's contribution level. Program is commercially viable without funder dependency. Funders buy access, not operations.

### Program Design

**Earth Education framework (head / heart / hands) - Meg's core expertise:**

| Week | Focus |
|------|-------|
| 1 | Sense of wonder. Ecological identity. Natural world as teacher. |
| 2 | Self-as-part-of-nature. Ecological identity in leadership. |
| 3 | Web of life. Systems thinking applied to the land. |
| 4 | Direct experience and solitude. Group self-manages. |
| 5 | Building caring communities. Collective action. |
| 6 | The full arc completes. Handover as an Earth Education act. |

*(Pilot uses weeks 1-3 only.)*

**Stewardship project:** Each cohort completes a specific on-land ecological project (revegetation, habitat, trail, water monitoring). The next cohort continues where the previous left off. Handover is through a documented pack + the physical project itself. Compounding legacy built cohort by cohort. Meg and Richard Z scope the pilot project by end of May.

**Alumni community:** Graduates pitch stewardship days, mentor current cohorts, run community events. Platform supports ongoing connection and leadership opportunity beyond the residency.
