Stream 1: Center Manager Role

Current State

The concept of a center manager (or program manager) role emerged from the 12 May strategy session as the operational glue for the three-layer model. Richard was candid: the current configuration relies on aging volunteers and informal coordination. As Wild Mountains transitions toward regular community events and a formal leadership program, a paid operational lead is necessary to make it work sustainably.

Richard marked the moment with some weight. Referring to the three young people at the table, he said: "It's the first time I have three people sitting here at this age group doing this stuff." The strategy session treated a funded role as the medium-term solution, with the EOY campaign providing partial initial funding.

Role Scope

The role was discussed in general terms. Based on the session:

  • Operational coordination of the WM property (volunteers, interns, backpackers)
  • Coordination of community events, working with subcontractors like Keely and Adam
  • Support for the Leadership Academy program delivery
  • Liaison between board decisions and day-to-day site activity

The exact FTE, job description, salary band, and reporting line were not finalised. These are open questions for the board to settle.

Funding Source

The EOY fundraising campaign (~$6,000 target) is partly directed at funding this role in the short term. Richard and the bookkeeper are reviewing unrestricted funds to determine whether existing reserves can contribute. The role's longer-term funding pathway beyond the initial campaign is not yet defined.

Gap note: The salary band, FTE, and whether this is the same position as "program manager" referenced in the EOY campaign or a distinct role were not resolved in the 12 May session. The morning session on 13 May (recording lost due to computer crash) may have clarified this. Verify with Kurt before the board meeting.

Board Decisions Needed

  1. Endorse the concept of a paid center/program manager role.
  2. Direct Richard to develop a job description, FTE estimate, and salary range for the next board meeting.
  3. Confirm whether the EOY campaign ~$6,000 target is earmarked for this role only, or split between the role and pilot cohort costs.

Risks

  • Without a paid operational lead, the three-layer model depends entirely on volunteer coordination, which has historically waned
  • Funding timeline: the role needs funding certainty before it can be advertised or filled

Stream 2: Leadership Academy

Current State

The Leadership Academy is the flagship program and the core mission layer of the three-layer model. The group unanimously agreed to move forward with a pilot. Kurt and Angelica are designing the program, with a concept document and budget due within 3 weeks.

Angelica framed the model well during the session: "You got your core product, which is the leadership program, which is like the mission. And then you've got this other layer which is the Wild Mountains community and experiences, and then the other layer is your venue hire." The board adopted this as the organising frame.

Curriculum Framework

The pilot structure as discussed:

  • Duration: 4 to 6 weeks (Kurt will include a recommendation in the concept document)
  • Cohort size: 5 to 6 participants
  • Capstone projects: one conservation project and one community event. Participants lead both end-to-end
  • Supervision model: tapering over the program. Higher support at the start, participants become more autonomous by the end
  • Format: immersive, on-site at Wild Mountains

The 4 vs 6 week question is still open. Both options have merit. The board should be ready to endorse one when the concept document arrives.

Participant Recruitment

Kurt will identify 5 to 6 potential participants from within the Wild Mountains community and through partner organisations including AYCC (Australian Youth Climate Coalition), ALCA, and Bush Heritage. A candidate shortlist is targeted within 4 weeks.

Pricing Tiers

Three tiers were proposed:

TierDescriptionPrice
VolunteerLong-term WM volunteers and community membersFree (50% discount on events)
CommunityStandard event attendees$80-$150 per event
Leadership ProgramPilot cohort participants~$1,200 fee per participant

The cost-per-participant for the pilot is approximately $3,200, meaning the philanthropic gap per participant is roughly $2,000. Across a cohort of 5 to 6 participants, total philanthropic subsidy needed is $10,000-$12,000.

Funding

The pilot is not designed to break even. It is a philanthropic investment. Funding sources discussed:

  • Individual participant fees (~$1,200 per person, ~$6,000-$7,200 for a full cohort)
  • EOY campaign contributions (partial)
  • Grant applications. Kurt has existing funder relationships via Co-Exist and Goodreach, including connections to VFFF, FRRR, and Sunshine Coast Council. Whether any have been specifically targeted for the Leadership Academy was not confirmed in the session
  • Partner sponsorships from AYCC, ALCA, Bush Heritage (potential co-investment in participants from their networks)

An alumni program (monthly Zoom calls, annual in-person gathering for graduates) was raised as a longer-term addition. This is a P3 item, not a board decision at this stage.

Board Decisions Needed

  1. Endorse the pilot Leadership Academy structure (4-6 weeks, 5-6 participants, two capstone projects).
  2. Note that Kurt and Angelica will deliver the concept document and budget within 3 weeks.
  3. Agree in principle to a fundraising target that covers the philanthropic gap (~$2,000 per participant across the cohort).

Risks

  • The concept document is the critical gate. If it slips beyond 3 weeks, the pilot timeline moves
  • Participant recruitment depends on Kurt's network and partner org outreach. Starting recruitment conversations alongside the design phase reduces timeline risk
  • The philanthropic subsidy gap of $10,000-$12,000 for a full pilot cohort is significant and requires dedicated outreach to funders

Stream 3: Branding and Events ("Wild Mountains Experiences")

Current State

The existing Wild Mountains Instagram carries a mix of volunteer work, environmental education, and event content. The group agreed this mix is confusing for the audiences Wild Mountains now wants to attract. The solution is a dedicated events brand.

Kurt is setting up the "Wild Mountains Experiences" Instagram page this week. Target: first post within 7 days, along with a 6-month events calendar.

Adam is coming to Wild Mountains for filming in late July, which creates a natural anchor for an August music event.

Subcontracting Model

Wild Mountains will not run every event internally. The agreed model:

  • Young organisers (Keely initially, Adam for music) design and deliver events
  • Wild Mountains provides the venue and logistical support
  • Revenue split: organiser retains 40-60% of ticket revenue
  • Organiser earns approximately $900 per event at current event scales
  • Wild Mountains retains 40-60% and the venue use

This model is intentionally a training and employment mechanism. It gives young people a paid pathway into event management while keeping the Wild Mountains team lean.

Pricing

Community events are priced at $80-$150 per person. The group discussed not undercharging. Kurt cited "Nature Fix" as an example of branding a nature experience at a premium. Cheap pricing, the group agreed, signals the wrong thing to the demographic Wild Mountains wants to attract.

Distribution and Marketing

The "Wild Mountains Experiences" Instagram is the primary marketing channel. Keely and Angelica will assist Kurt with content. Video content for both the EOY campaign and the new Instagram was filmed on 13 May 2026 (Richard testimonial, b-roll of the property, group team shot with Kurt, Angelica, Tate, and Keely).

A website or landing page for Wild Mountains Experiences, with event listings and ticket booking, is a longer-term requirement. Not scoped for immediate action.

Board Decisions Needed

  1. Note and endorse the "Wild Mountains Experiences" Instagram brand launch (Kurt, this week).
  2. Confirm the subcontracting model: organiser earns ~$900 + 40-60% revenue share, Wild Mountains provides venue and support.

Risks

  • Consistent content is the challenge for a new Instagram page. If Kurt is stretched, the page risks going quiet after the launch posts. A simple 90-day content calendar helps manage this
  • The first paid event (Keely, late August/September) is the proof-of-concept for the subcontracting model. If it underperforms financially, the model needs adjustment before scaling

Cross-Stream Dependencies

The four streams are not independent. Key linkages:

  1. Leadership Academy depends on Center Manager being in place. Without an operational lead, running a 4-6 week immersive program for 5-6 participants is not feasible at current staffing levels.
  1. EOY campaign funds the Center Manager role. The ~$6,000 campaign target is explicitly linked to the program manager role and pilot cohort costs. These two streams are financially co-dependent.
  1. Community events feed the Leadership Academy. Events are the entry point for young people who may later become leadership program participants. Keely and Adam are both potential candidates for this pipeline, not just event organisers.
  1. Branding clarity enables all three other streams. The "Wild Mountains Experiences" Instagram is the front door for community members. Without clear branding, recruitment for events, the leadership program, and volunteers remains fragmented.
  1. Financial clarity gates everything. Until Richard confirms unrestricted funds available, the EOY campaign target, the program manager salary, and the pilot cohort subsidy are all estimates. The financial review is the most time-critical item on the board's agenda.
Sequence implication: Richard's financial review (due within 2 weeks) is the critical path item. Everything else can proceed in parallel, but the fundraising campaign cannot finalise its target, and the program manager decision cannot be made, until the financials are clear.
