Stream 1: Centre Manager Role

The Urgency

Meg leaves October 2025. To replace her in time, the timeline is rigid:

  • 19 May: Job listing posted
  • Mid-June: Applications close, interviews run
  • 1 July: CM hired and starts
  • July-October: 4-week Meg handover runs before she departs

If the listing slips even a few weeks, the Meg handover window shrinks or disappears entirely. This is the most time-sensitive action item in this report.

Role Design

Meg's insight from the Day 2 session was clear: the current role is really two roles.

RoleFocusTiming
Centre Manager (CM)Business: bookings, financials, suppliers, volunteers, accommodation, complianceHire by 1 July 2025
Program Manager (PM)Education: curriculum, facilitation, participant experienceYear 2 hire

The CM is the immediate priority. Meg covers the educational facilitation during the August pilot as a volunteer.

CM responsibilities:

  • Day-to-day operational coordination of the WM property (volunteers, interns, backpackers)
  • Community events coordination (working with Keeley, Adam, and other subcontractors)
  • Accommodation management
  • Liaison between board decisions and site activity
  • 30% of time allocated to Leadership Residency program support (~10-15 hours/week)

Salary: $80,000. Live on-site (accommodation included).

Knowledge Transfer

Tate (Ecodia) leads the technical architecture for knowledge transfer directly with Meg. Starts immediately.

Architecture:

  • Layer 1: Word documents organised by function (8 domains: site ops, program delivery, staffing, accommodation, bookings, finance/admin, compliance, community)
  • Layer 2: AI assistant trained on those documents - new CM can ask natural language questions ("what do I need to onboard an intern?") and be directed to the right information

Trello is the capture tool during extraction sessions with Meg. Word docs are the durable home. The AI bot is the retrieval interface for whoever comes next.

Blue Card compliance knowledge is a good example of what this captures: currently in Meg's head, needs to live in a document the new CM can find in 30 seconds.

Funder model: External funders pay $4,200 so a participant who can't afford full price doesn't have to. Wild Mountains receives the same revenue regardless of whether the funder contributes nothing, part, or all. The program is commercially viable without funder dependency. Funders buy access, not operations.

Program Design

Earth Education framework (head / heart / hands) runs through each week:

WeekFocus
1Sense of wonder, ecological identity - the natural world as teacher
2Self-as-part-of-nature, ecological identity in leadership
3Web of life, systems thinking applied to the land
4Direct experience and solitude - group self-manages
5Building caring communities, collective action
6The full arc completes - handover as an Earth Education act

Pilot uses weeks 1-3 only

Stewardship Project: Each cohort completes a specific ecological project on the land (revegetation, habitat, trail, water monitoring). The next cohort continues where the previous left off. Handover is through a documented handover pack and the project itself - no person-to-person overlap needed. Compounding legacy built cohort by cohort.

Cohort sequencing: Sequential only, with 3-4 week gaps between cohorts for staff rest, site reset, and admin. No overlapping cohorts.

Participant intake (TAFE-style):

  1. EOI online form (15 min, Humanitix or Typeform)
  2. Shortlisting by program manager (1 week)
  3. 20-minute informal interview (video or phone)
  4. Offer + 7-day acceptance window
  5. Enrolment: program agreement, medical form, payment
  6. Pre-arrival platform onboarding (2 weeks before arrival)

Impact Tracking Platform

A proper purpose-built platform - not Notion or Google Forms. This is a joint initiative across the core team (Meg, Tate, Kurt, Richard Z, Angelica). Tate (Ecodia) handles the technical build.

Pilot version (by mid-July):

  • Weekly reflection prompts + confidence check-ins
  • Stewardship project log with photos
  • Simple enough to use from day one of the August pilot

Full build (year 2):

  • Confidence and nature connectedness tracking over time
  • Stewardship project log with photos and measurements
  • Funder dashboard (anonymised, aggregate data)
  • Alumni community - graduates stay connected, post updates, mentor current cohorts, pitch stewardship projects
  • 3-month and 12-month graduate check-ins

  • Volunteer preparation - program context, safety, cultural and ecological background
  • Program pre-arrival - Leadership Residency participants complete structured modules in the 2 weeks before arrival
  • Future expansion - supports other roles and programs as WM grows

This is an Ecodia deliverable. Timeline and scope to be confirmed (see Items for Tate to Clarify before Sending).
