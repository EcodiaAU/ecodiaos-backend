3. Part A — Resonaverde Referring Clients to Ecodia

Mechanism unchanged from v2. Resonaverde refers clients seeking Ecodia Services. Ecodia pays commission.

3.1 Right of First Refusal

Where Resonaverde identifies a client seeking AI, software or programming services, Resonaverde will first offer Ecodia a right of first refusal, notifying Ecodia in accordance with clause 3.2. Ecodia will respond within 5 business days. If Ecodia declines or does not respond, Resonaverde may refer that client elsewhere and no Commission is payable.

3.2 Referral Process

To qualify as a Referred Client (A), Resonaverde must submit a referral notice (email sufficient) to Ecodia before Ecodia first contacts that client in relation to the Ecodia Services. The notice must identify the client, primary contact, and nature of services anticipated. A client is only a Referred Client (A) once Ecodia has confirmed receipt and accepted the referral.

3.3 Part A Commission

Ecodia pays Resonaverde a Part A Commission on revenue received from Referred Clients (A), on the GST-exclusive amount invoiced and paid:

Revenue typeCommission rate
Upfront / project fees15%
Ongoing retainer / maintenance / recurring10%

Commission applies for 24 months from Ecodia's first invoice to that client. No Commission on: pass-through costs, GST, refunds, bad debts, or non-Referred Clients.

5. Invoicing, Payment and Reporting
5.1 Monthly Reporting — Both Directions

Within 5 business days of end of each calendar month: (a) Ecodia provides Resonaverde a statement of Part A Commissions; (b) Resonaverde provides Ecodia a statement of Part B Commissions. Each statement shows: revenue received from Referred Clients, commissions accrued, any carried forward, and total payable.

5.2 Invoicing and Payment — Both Directions

(a) Resonaverde invoices Ecodia for Part A Commissions payable. Ecodia pays within 14 days.
(b) Ecodia invoices Resonaverde for Part B Commissions payable. Resonaverde pays within 14 days.
Where the paying Party is GST-registered, a valid tax invoice is required and GST is payable in addition.

5.3 Audit Right — Both Directions

Each Party may, no more than once per calendar quarter, request a breakdown of invoices issued by the other Party to Referred Clients. The responding Party replies within 10 business days. If a material error (>5% of Commission payable) is found in the requesting Party's favour, the erring Party bears the audit cost and pays the shortfall promptly.

7. Term and Termination
7.1 Term

This Agreement commences on the date it is signed by both Parties and continues until terminated.

7.2 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate for any reason by giving at least 30 days' written notice.

7.3 Termination for Breach or Insolvency

Either Party may terminate immediately if the other Party: (a) commits a material breach and fails to remedy it within 14 days of written notice; or (b) becomes insolvent, enters administration or liquidation.

7.4 Effect of Termination on Commissions

Termination for convenience, or by the non-breaching Party for breach: Both Part A and Part B Commissions continue for Referred Clients introduced before termination, on revenue received during a 6-month tail from the termination date, subject to the 24-month per-client cap.

Termination triggered by a Party's own breach or insolvency: That Party's outgoing Commissions cease on termination. Commissions owed to the non-breaching Party are unaffected.

Commissions accrued and unpaid at termination remain payable per clause 5.

8. Mutual Obligations

Each Party will: (a) act in good faith; (b) not make false or misleading representations about the other Party; (c) comply with all applicable laws including privacy and spam laws; and (d) keep the other Party's confidential information confidential and use it only for the purposes of this Agreement.

9. GST and Taxes

All amounts are GST-exclusive. Where GST applies, the recipient pays GST in addition to the stated amount on receipt of a valid tax invoice. Each Party is responsible for its own income tax obligations.

10. Relationship of the Parties

The Parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, agency, employment or fiduciary relationship.

11. Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by law, neither Party is liable for indirect, consequential, special or incidental loss, or loss of profit, revenue, goodwill, business opportunity or data.

Each Party's total aggregate liability (other than for unpaid Commissions) is limited to the greater of: (a) total Commissions paid or payable by that Party in the 12 months preceding the event giving rise to the claim; or (b) AUD $1,000.

Nothing in this Agreement excludes liability that cannot lawfully be excluded, including under the Australian Consumer Law.

12. General

12.1 Entire agreement. This Agreement supersedes Referral Agreement v2 (20 April 2026) and all prior understandings on its subject matter. Separate engagements (including any future CETN build) are governed by their own signed documents.

12.2 Variation. Any variation must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

12.3 Assignment. Neither Party may assign rights without prior written consent of the other, not to be unreasonably withheld. Assignment to a related entity under common ownership is permitted with prior written notice and the assignee's written agreement to be bound.

12.4 Governing law. Queensland, Australia. Non-exclusive jurisdiction of Queensland courts.

12.5 Notices. In writing by email. Addresses: Ecodia — code@ecodia.au; Resonaverde — hello@resonaverde.au.

12.6 Counterparts. May be signed in counterparts, including electronically.
